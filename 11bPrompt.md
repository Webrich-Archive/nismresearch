### Chapter-by-Chapter Content Guide (This Session)

**Module 3: Investment Products (30 marks — 20% weight)**

- **Ch 7 (pp 127-133):** Investment types overview — equity, fixed income, commodities, real estate, gold. Short chapter, mostly conceptual framework for the detailed chapters that follow.
- **Ch 8 (pp 134-151):** Equity investing — fundamental analysis (P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, DCF, DDM), technical analysis basics, risks of equity. **CALCULATION QUESTIONS from valuation models.** Key formulas:
  - P/E ratio = Market Price / EPS
  - P/B ratio = Market Price / Book Value per share
  - EV/EBITDA = (Market Cap + Debt - Cash) / EBITDA
  - DDM: P = D1 / (r - g) where D1 = D0 × (1+g)
  - DCF basics: sum of discounted future cash flows
  - Earnings yield = EPS / Market Price (inverse of P/E)
- **Ch 9 (pp 152-184):** Fixed income — bond pricing, YTM, current yield, duration, convexity, yield curve, money market instruments, G-Secs, corporate bonds, small savings (PPF, NSC, SCSS). **CALCULATION HEAVY — bond math.** Key formulas:
  - Bond price = Σ C/(1+r)^t + F/(1+r)^n
  - Current yield = Annual Coupon / Current Market Price
  - YTM approximation = [C + (F-P)/n] / [(F+P)/2]
  - Duration = Σ [t × PV(CFt)] / Bond Price (Macaulay duration)
  - Modified duration = Macaulay Duration / (1 + YTM/k)
  - Price change ≈ -Modified Duration × Δy × Price
- **Ch 10 (pp 185-198):** Derivatives basics — forwards, futures, options, put-call parity, hedging vs speculation, index futures/options. Lighter than Series VIII but covers concepts. Key concepts:
  - Forwards vs Futures (customized vs standardized)
  - Call and Put options (right to buy vs right to sell)
  - Put-Call Parity: C + PV(K) = P + S
  - Intrinsic value vs time value
  - Hedging vs speculation vs arbitrage

**Module 4: Investment Through Managed Portfolios (23 marks — 15% weight)**

- **Ch 11 (pp 199-224):** Mutual funds — structure (sponsor, trustee, AMC), types (equity/debt/hybrid/solution-oriented/other), SIP/STP/SWP, NAV calculation, expense ratio, exit load, direct vs regular plans. Key formulas:
  - NAV = (Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Number of Units Outstanding
  - Expense ratio impact on returns
  - SIP returns (FV of annuity)
- **Ch 12 (pp 225-232):** Portfolio Management Services (PMS) — types (discretionary/non-discretionary/advisory), registration requirements, minimum investment Rs. 50 lakh, fee structures (fixed fee, profit sharing, hybrid).
- **Ch 13 (pp 233-243):** Alternative Investment Funds — Cat I (VC, SME, social venture, infra), Cat II (PE, debt), Cat III (hedge funds), minimum investment Rs. 1 crore, angel funds Rs. 25 lakh, key regulatory provisions.
