Q22: Grandfathering -- Deemed Cost of Acquisition
ISSUE: Bought at Rs. 80, NAV on 31 Jan 2018 = Rs. 120, sold at Rs. 100.
Per PDF (page 157): "Gains would be calculated for the purpose of tax by taking
the higher of the purchase price or the NAV as on January 31, 2018."

Capital loss = 100 - 120 = -20 (loss).
But the answer (C) says "Rs. 100 (NAV on 31 Jan 2018 capped at sale price since
sale price is lower)." This is the CORRECT treatment per the Income Tax rules --
when the sale price is between cost and Jan 31, 2018 NAV, the sale price itself
is taken as the deemed cost (to ensure no artificial loss is created).
This is verified: per IT rules, cost of acquisition = min(NAV on 31 Jan 2018,
max(actual cost, sale price)) = min(120, max(80, 100)) = min(120, 100) = 100.
So the capital gain = 100 - 100 = 0. CONFIRMED CORRECT.
Note: The PDF's wording ("higher of purchase price or NAV on January 31, 2018")
is simplified and doesn't fully capture the capping rule. The question correctly
applies the full IT Act rule. CONFIRMED FINE.
