### Chapter-by-Chapter Content Guide

**Module 1: Personal Financial Planning (37 marks — HEAVIEST MODULE)**
- **Ch 1 (pp 18-30):** Financial planning concept, need, scope, 6-step process, assets/liabilities/net worth, advisory vs execution
- **Ch 2 (pp 31-43):** Time Value of Money — PV, FV, annuities (ordinary vs due), perpetuity, NPV, IRR, CAGR, real vs nominal returns, rule of 72. **CALCULATION HEAVY — this is the core math chapter**
- **Ch 3 (pp 44-63):** Cash flow management, household budgets, personal balance sheet, net worth, contingency planning, financial ratios (savings ratio, debt-to-income, liquidity ratio)
- **Ch 4 (pp 64-87):** Debt management — types of loans (home/car/personal/education), EMI calculation, fixed vs floating rates, amortization, loan restructuring, debt reduction strategies

**Module 2: Indian Financial Markets (10 marks)**
- **Ch 5 (pp 89-103):** Indian economy overview, financial markets structure, regulators (SEBI, RBI, IRDAI, PFRDA), money market vs capital market
- **Ch 6 (pp 104-125):** Primary markets (IPO/FPO/rights/QIP), secondary markets (exchanges, trading, settlement T+1), corporate actions (bonus, split, dividend, rights)

**Module 3: Investment Products (30 marks)**
- **Ch 7 (pp 127-133):** Investment types overview — equity, fixed income, commodities, real estate, gold
- **Ch 8 (pp 134-151):** Equity investing — fundamental analysis (P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, DCF, DDM), technical analysis basics, risks of equity. **CALCULATION QUESTIONS from valuation models**
- **Ch 9 (pp 152-184):** Fixed income — bond pricing, YTM, current yield, duration, convexity, yield curve, money market instruments, G-Secs, corporate bonds, small savings (PPF, NSC, SCSS). **CALCULATION HEAVY — bond math**
- **Ch 10 (pp 185-198):** Derivatives basics — forwards, futures, options, put-call parity, hedging vs speculation, index futures/options. Lighter than Series VIII but covers concepts.

**Module 4: Investment Through Managed Portfolios (23 marks)**
- **Ch 11 (pp 199-224):** Mutual funds — structure, types (equity/debt/hybrid/solution-oriented), SIP/STP/SWP, NAV calculation, expense ratio, exit load, direct vs regular plans
- **Ch 12 (pp 225-232):** Portfolio Management Services (PMS) — types (discretionary/non-discretionary/advisory), registration, minimum investment Rs. 50 lakh, fees structure
- **Ch 13 (pp 233-243):** AIFs — Cat I (VC, SME, social venture, infra), Cat II (PE, debt), Cat III (hedge funds), minimum investment Rs. 1 crore, angel funds Rs. 25 lakh

**Module 5: Portfolio Construction, Performance Monitoring and Evaluation (20 marks)**
- **Ch 14 (pp 244-250):** Modern Portfolio Theory — Markowitz, efficient frontier, systematic vs unsystematic risk, beta, correlation, diversification
- **Ch 15 (pp 251-265):** Portfolio construction — asset allocation (strategic vs tactical), risk profiling, life cycle analysis, rebalancing, benchmarking
- **Ch 16 (pp 266-289):** Performance measurement — Sharpe ratio, Treynor ratio, Jensen's alpha, information ratio, tracking error, TWRR vs MWRR, benchmark comparison. **CALCULATION HEAVY**

**Module 6: Operations, Regulatory Environment, Compliance and Ethics (30 marks)**
- **Ch 17 (pp 291-333):** Operational aspects — KYC, PAN, demat, PoA, NRI account opening, folio consolidation, payment instruments
- **Ch 18 (pp 334-366):** Key regulations — SCRA, SEBI Act, PFUTP, Intermediaries Regs, PIT Regs, **SEBI Investment Advisers Regulations 2013** (registration, net worth, qualifications, fee structure, client agreement, risk profiling, suitability, disclosure). **This is the most important regulatory chapter for this exam.**
- **Ch 19 (pp 367-376):** Ethics — fiduciary duty, conflicts of interest, churning, front-running, mis-selling, suitability obligations
- **Ch 20 (pp 377-386):** Grievance redress — Consumer Protection Act, SCORES, SEBI complaints, banking/insurance/pension ombudsman, SAT

### Key Calculation Areas (Must be covered)

1. **TVM (Ch 2):** PV, FV, annuity (ordinary + due), perpetuity, NPV, IRR, CAGR, real return = (1+nominal)/(1+inflation) - 1, Rule of 72
2. **EMI/Loans (Ch 4):** EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n - 1], amortization schedules, impact of rate changes on EMI vs tenure
3. **Equity Valuation (Ch 8):** P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, DDM (P = D1/(r-g)), DCF basics, earnings yield
4. **Bond Math (Ch 9):** Bond price = Σ C/(1+r)^t + F/(1+r)^n, current yield = coupon/price, YTM approximation, duration, modified duration
5. **Portfolio Math (Ch 14-16):** Portfolio return = Σ wi×ri, portfolio variance (2-asset), Sharpe = (Rp-Rf)/σp, Treynor = (Rp-Rf)/βp, Jensen's α = Rp - [Rf + β(Rm-Rf)], tracking error, information ratio
6. **MF Calculations (Ch 11):** NAV = (total assets - liabilities) / units, expense ratio impact, SIP returns
