SECTION 1: CORRECTIONS NEEDED
================================================================================

1. Mutual Funds_25.xml - CONCEPT NOT EXPLICITLY IN PDF (MINOR)
   ---------------------------------------------------------------
   Question: "Compared to a regular plan, a direct plan of the same mutual fund
   scheme will have: A lower expense ratio and higher NAV"
   Issue: The PDF (Chapter 5) does NOT explicitly discuss "direct plans" vs
   "regular plans." The PDF mentions that "Investors can also choose to invest
   directly in a fund without involving a distributor" (page 169) but does not
   explain that direct plans have lower TER or higher NAV. The concept itself
   is correct in practice, but the question goes beyond what is stated in
   Chapter 5 of this workbook.
   Recommendation: The question is factually correct per general SEBI norms,
   but the explanation should note that this concept is not explicitly covered
   in this specific chapter. Low priority -- acceptable to keep as-is since it
   is common MF knowledge and consistent with SEBI circulars.

2. Mutual Funds_30.xml - MINOR THRESHOLD DISCREPANCY
   ---------------------------------------------------------------
   Question: States diversification possible with "Rs. 5,000"
   PDF Reference: The PDF states "even with a small investment of say, Rs.5000"
   (page 167) for diversification. HOWEVER, elsewhere (page 190) the PDF also
   says "sometimes as low as Rs. 500" for mutual fund investment. The question
   explanation uses Rs. 5,000 which matches the diversification context. The
   benefits section on page 190 says Rs. 500.
   Recommendation: No correction needed for the question itself, but the
   explanation could note both thresholds -- Rs. 500 is the minimum possible
   investment mentioned, while Rs. 5,000 is the specific diversification
   context reference.

3. Mutual Funds_32.xml - NFO PERIOD STATED AS 15 DAYS (VERIFY)
   ---------------------------------------------------------------
   Question explanation states: "The NFO (except ELSS) will be open for a
   period of 15 days."
   PDF Reference: Page 183 confirms "The NFO (except ELSS) will be open for a
   period of 15 days" -- this matches exactly.
   Status: CONFIRMED CORRECT. No correction needed.

4. Mutual Funds_46.xml - MINOR WORDING: BORROWING RESTRICTION
   ---------------------------------------------------------------
   Question explanation states: "Mutual funds in India are not permitted to
   borrow to invest."
   PDF Reference: Page 162 footnote 55 states more precisely: "a mutual fund
   may borrow only to meet temporary liquidity needs, such as for repurchase
   or redemption of units, or for payment of interest or dividend to
   unitholders." So mutual funds CAN borrow, but ONLY for temporary liquidity
   needs, NOT for investment purposes.
   Recommendation: The explanation's statement is essentially correct in
   spirit (cannot borrow to invest) but should be more precise. Suggest
   revising to: "Mutual funds in India may borrow only to meet temporary
   liquidity needs such as redemptions, not to invest in a portfolio."

SECTION 3: CONCEPT GAPS
(Important PDF content NOT covered by any of the 50 questions)
================================================================================

HIGH PRIORITY GAPS:

1. FLEXI CAP FUND (Section 5.3.1, page 172)
   The PDF defines Flexi Cap Fund: minimum 65% equity, invests across market
   caps dynamically. Multi Cap is covered (Q41) but Flexi Cap is NOT covered
   by any question, despite being a key distinction from Multi Cap.

2. LARGE AND MID-CAP FUND (Section 5.3.1, page 172)
   PDF specifies: minimum 35% each in large cap and mid cap equity. This
   specific hybrid-cap category is not tested by any question.

3. MID CAP FUND / SMALL CAP FUND THRESHOLDS (Section 5.3.1, page 172)
   PDF specifies minimum 65% equity in mid/small cap companies respectively.
   These specific allocation requirements are not tested.

4. CORPORATE BOND FUND VS CREDIT RISK FUND (Section 5.3.2, page 175)
   PDF details: Corporate Bond Fund = 80% in AA+ and above. Credit Risk Fund
   = 65% in AA and below (excluding AA+). This important distinction between
   investment-grade debt fund types is not covered.

5. FIXED MATURITY PLANS (FMP) (Section 5.4.1, page 179)
   PDF covers FMPs as closed-ended funds where securities and scheme mature
   together. Primary risk is credit risk. Not tested by any question.

6. INFRASTRUCTURE DEBT FUNDS (Section 5.4.2, page 179)
   PDF details: closed-ended, minimum 5-year tenor, 90% in infra debt
   securities, minimum investment Rs. 1 crore, face value Rs. 10 lakh per
   unit. Not tested.

7. REAL ESTATE MUTUAL FUNDS (Section 5.4.3, page 179)
   PDF details: at least 35% in physical assets, not less than 75% in
   physical assets and related securities, valued every 90 days by two valuers.
   Not tested.

8. SPECIALIZED INVESTMENT FUND (SIF) (Section 5.13, page 193)
   PDF introduces SIF: minimum Rs. 10 lakhs investment, new product line
   under MF regulations. This is a NEW concept (introduced Dec 2024) not
   covered by any question.

9. SILVER ETFs (Section 5.3.5, page 178)
   PDF mentions introduction of Silver ETFs in Indian markets. Gold ETF is
   covered (Q16) but Silver ETF is not tested.

10. DEBT FUND DURATION CATEGORIES (Section 5.3.2, pages 174-176)
    PDF provides detailed Macaulay duration ranges for each debt fund type:
    - Ultra Short Duration: 3-6 months
    - Low Duration: 6-12 months
    - Short Duration: 1-3 years
    - Medium Duration: 3-4 years
    - Medium to Long Duration: 4-7 years
    - Long Duration: >7 years
    - Overnight Fund: 1 day maturity
    Only Liquid Fund (91 days) is tested in Q13. The full spectrum of debt
    fund duration categories is a significant gap.

MEDIUM PRIORITY GAPS:

11. SWITCH TRANSACTION (Section 5.8, page 189)
    PDF defines switch as a combined redemption + purchase on a given date,
    distinct from STP (which is a series of transactions). Not tested.

12. BALANCED HYBRID FUND (Section 5.3.3, page 176)
    PDF specifies: 40%-60% equity and 40%-60% debt, no arbitrage permitted.
    Mutual funds can offer either Aggressive Hybrid or Balanced, not both.
    Only Aggressive Hybrid is tested (Q14).

13. CONSERVATIVE HYBRID FUND (Section 5.3.3, page 176)
    PDF specifies: 75%-90% debt, 10%-25% equity. Not directly tested.

14. MULTI ASSET ALLOCATION (Section 5.3.3, page 177)
    PDF specifies: minimum 3 asset classes, minimum 10% each, foreign
    securities NOT treated as separate asset class. Not tested.

15. ARBITRAGE FUND (Section 5.3.3, page 177)
    PDF describes: minimum 65% equity, exploits price differential between
    cash and derivatives markets. Low volatility similar to liquid fund.
    Not tested.

16. VALUE FUND / CONTRA FUND (Section 5.3.1, page 172)
    PDF specifies: MFs can offer EITHER value fund OR contra fund, not both.
    Minimum 65% equity. Not tested.

17. SECTORAL / THEMATIC FUND (Section 5.3.1, page 173)
    PDF specifies: minimum 80% in sector/theme equity. Not tested.

18. ESG INVESTING SUB-CATEGORY (Section 5.3.1, page 173)
    PDF lists 6 ESG strategies (Exclusion, Integration, Best-in-class,
    Impact investing, Sustainable objectives, Transition). Not tested.

19. ACTIVE VS PASSIVE INVESTING (Section 5.5, pages 179-181)
    While tracking error is tested (Q40) and ETF mechanics (Q15), the
    broader concept of active vs passive investing, and the iNAV disclosure
    for ETFs, and the Rs. 25 crore threshold for direct ETF transactions
    with AMCs, are not tested.

20. FATCA AND CRS COMPLIANCE (Section 5.6.3, page 182)
    PDF covers FATCA/CRS obligations, 30-day reporting requirement for
    status changes. Not tested by any question.

21. PAYMENT INSTRUMENTS (Section 5.6.4, page 185)
    PDF specifies cash payment limit of Rs. 50,000 per MF house for
    purchases. Not tested.

22. JOINT HOLDERS (Section 5.6.4, page 185)
    PDF specifies: up to 3 holders, joint vs either-or-survivor modes.
    Not tested.

23. FOLIO AND STATEMENT OF ACCOUNTS (Section 5.6.7, page 186-187)
    PDF details: CAS sent monthly for transaction months, 6-monthly for
    non-transaction folios. Investors can register up to 5 bank accounts.
    Not tested.

24. DISTRIBUTOR COMMISSION - TRAIL MODEL (Section 5.6.8, page 187)
    PDF specifies: full trail model, no upfront commission, upfronting
    only for SIP inflows (subject to conditions). Not tested.

25. GILT FUND / GILT FUND 10-YEAR CONSTANT DURATION (Section 5.3.2, p176)
    PDF specifies: minimum 80% in G-secs, Macaulay duration equal to 10
    years for constant duration variant. Not tested.

26. SCHEME EXCEPTIONS TO ONE-PER-CATEGORY RULE (Section 5.2, page 163)
    PDF states exceptions: (a) Index Funds/ETFs tracking different indices,
    (b) FoFs with different underlying schemes, (c) Sectoral/thematic
    investing in different sectors/themes. Not directly tested.

27. NFO ALLOTMENT FOR UNITS IN 5 BUSINESS DAYS / INVESTOR SERVICE
    STANDARDS (Section 5.12, page 192-193)
    PDF details allotment within 5 business days, daily NAV disclosure,
    periodic account statements. Q49 covers redemption timeline but NFO
    allotment and dividend dispatch timelines are not tested separately.

28. MUTUAL FUND VOTING OBLIGATIONS (Section 5.12, page 193)
    PDF states SEBI mandates MFs to vote on specified resolutions at
    company general meetings (corporate governance, capital structure,
    management compensation, related party transactions). Not tested.

29. PASSIVE ELSS (Section 5.5.2, page 181)
    PDF mentions MFs can launch Passive ELSS under "Other Schemes" based
    on indices comprising top 250 companies. Not tested.

30. OVERNIGHT FUND (Section 5.3.2, page 174)
    PDF defines: investing in overnight securities with 1-day maturity.
    Not tested.

identified are clarification-level, not factual errors. The most significant
finding is the 30 concept gaps -- several important PDF topics (Flexi Cap,

and many hybrid fund sub-types) have no corresponding questions. The
question bank heavily favors conceptual/definitional questions and covers
the most prominent topics well, but deeper regulatory details and the full
spectrum of fund types remain untested.
