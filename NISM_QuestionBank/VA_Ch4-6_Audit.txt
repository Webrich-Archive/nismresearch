CRITICAL CORRECTIONS (Factually wrong or misleading answers)

1. Fund Distribution Q6: Transaction Charge for New Investor
   Question: "What is the transaction charge payable to a mutual fund distributor
   for a purchase transaction of Rs. 10,000 or more from a new investor?"
   Current Correct Answer: "Rs. 150"

   PROBLEM: The November 2025 PDF (page 126-127, Section 6.5.3) explicitly states
   that SEBI's circular issued in August 2025 WITHDREW the transaction charges
   framework entirely. The PDF says: "this transaction charges framework has been
   withdrawn, thereby removing the following: The investor-borne transaction charges,
   The requirement for investors to pay upfront commissions to distributors."

   RECOMMENDATION: This question must be either REMOVED or REWRITTEN to test
   knowledge of the withdrawal. For example: "What is the current status of
   transaction charges payable by investors to mutual fund distributors?" with
   correct answer: "SEBI withdrew the transaction charges framework in August 2025;
   no transaction charges are deducted from investor contributions."

2. Fund Distribution Q15: Transaction Charge Below Rs. 10,000
   Question: "What happens to the transaction charge when an investor invests less
   than Rs. 10,000 through a distributor?"
   Current Correct Answer: "No transaction charge is applicable for investments
   below Rs. 10,000"

   PROBLEM: Same as above. The entire transaction charges framework has been
   withdrawn per the PDF. The question tests an obsolete framework.

   RECOMMENDATION: Remove or rewrite.

3. Fund Distribution Q35: Net Amount After Transaction Charge
   Question: "An existing mutual fund investor invests Rs. 25,000 through a
   distributor in a Regular Plan. What is the net amount that gets invested in the
   scheme after accounting for the transaction charge?"
   Current Correct Answer: "Rs. 24,900 (after deducting Rs. 100 transaction charge
   for existing investors)"

   PROBLEM: Same as Q6 and Q15. Transaction charges have been withdrawn. The
   correct answer under current regulations is Rs. 25,000 (full amount invested,
   no deduction). The question and all its options are based on the old framework.

   RECOMMENDATION: Remove or rewrite.

4. Fund Distribution Q38 (Case Study): Total Transaction Charges
   Question: Calculates total transaction charges of Rs. 250 (Rs. 150 + Rs. 100).
   Current Correct Answer: "Rs. 250 (Rs. 150 from Mr. Sharma as a new investor +
   Rs. 100 from Mrs. Gupta as an existing investor)"

   PROBLEM: Same as above. Transaction charges framework withdrawn.

   RECOMMENDATION: Remove or rewrite the case study question.

5. Legal Q29: NAV Decimal Places
   Question: "Under SEBI regulations, how should the NAV of a mutual fund scheme
   be rounded off for computation purposes?"
   Current Correct Answer: "Up to four decimal places"

   PROBLEM: The PDF (page 150, Section 7.5) states:
   - "NAV is to be calculated up to 4 decimal places in the case of INDEX FUNDS,
     LIQUID FUNDS and OTHER DEBT FUNDS."
   - "NAV for EQUITY and BALANCED funds is to be calculated up to at least 2
     decimal places."
   The answer "up to four decimal places" is NOT universally correct. It applies
   only to specific fund types, not all schemes.

   RECOMMENDATION: Reword the question to specify fund type, or change the
   correct answer to reflect the split rule. E.g., "Which of the following is true
   about NAV rounding?" with answer "4 decimal places for index, liquid and debt
   funds; at least 2 decimal places for equity and balanced funds."

6. Fund Distribution Q5: Additional TER for B30 Cities
   Question: "What is the additional TER that AMCs are allowed to charge for
   inflows from B30 cities?"
   Current Correct Answer: "30 basis points"

   PROBLEM: The November 2025 PDF does NOT specify "30 basis points" anywhere.
   The PDF (page 146-147) says additional expenses "in the manner as specified by
   SEBI from time to time" and references a November 2025 SEBI circular on
   "additional incentives to distributors for onboarding new individual investors
   from B-30 cities and women investors." The old 30 bps rule under Regulation
   52(6A)(b) has been restructured. The workbook deliberately avoids specifying
   the exact number, saying it is "as specified by SEBI from time to time."

   RECOMMENDATION: The question should be rewritten to match the PDF's framing.
   The answer should acknowledge the additional incentive exists but the exact
   amount is "as specified by SEBI from time to time." Alternatively, if the
   Nov 2025 SEBI circular does specify 30 bps, cite that circular explicitly in
   the explanation. As it stands, the number is not verifiable from the workbook.

7. Legal Q10: Sponsor's Contribution to AMC Capital
   Question: "What is the minimum percentage of the AMC's net worth that a sponsor
   must contribute under SEBI Mutual Fund Regulations?"
   Current Correct Answer: "40%"

   ISSUE: The PDF (Chapter 3, pages 60-65) discusses sponsor requirements but does
   NOT explicitly state "40% of AMC net worth." The PDF states the sponsor must:
   - Have positive net worth in preceding 5 years
   - Have positive liquid net worth more than the proposed capital contribution
   - Have net profit of at least Rs. 10 crore average over 5 years
   The 40% figure comes from SEBI MF Regulations Regulation 7(c) which states the
   sponsor shall contribute not less than 40% of the net worth of the AMC, but this
   specific percentage is NOT stated in the workbook text within Chapters 3-6.

   ASSESSMENT: The answer is factually correct per SEBI regulations but not
   explicitly stated in the workbook. This is acceptable for exam purposes since
   candidates are expected to know the regulations. No change needed but the
   explanation should note this comes from Regulation 7(c).

8. Legal Q27: Interest Rate for Delayed Redemption Proceeds
   Question: "Under SEBI regulations, what happens if a mutual fund AMC fails to
   dispatch redemption proceeds within 10 business days?"
   Current Correct Answer: "The AMC must pay interest at 15% per annum to the
   investor for the period of delay"

   ISSUE: The PDF (page 217, footnote 94) says for redemption/repurchase proceeds:
   "the AMC has to pay the unit-holder, interest at the rate as may be specified
   by SEBI for the period of such delay." It does NOT specify 15%.
   The 15% figure is explicitly stated only for NFO refund delays (page 217,
   footnote 93 and page 170). The question also says "10 business days" but the
   PDF says "within such period as may be specified by the Board from time to time."

   ASSESSMENT: The 15% and 10-business-day figures come from SEBI circulars and
   are generally accepted as correct. However, the workbook itself uses more
   general language. Consider adding a note in the explanation that the specific
   figures come from SEBI circulars rather than the workbook text.

9. Legal Q14: Minimum 20 Investors
   Question: "Under SEBI regulations, what is the minimum number of investors
   required in a mutual fund scheme after the initial NFO period?"
   Current Correct Answer: "20 investors"

   ISSUE: The PDF mentions "Provisions regarding the minimum number of investors
   in the scheme" as a SID section heading (page 96) but does NOT explicitly state
   the number "20" in Chapters 4-6. The number 20 comes from SEBI Regulation
   39(1). The answer is factually correct per regulations.

   ASSESSMENT: Acceptable. No change needed.

10. Legal Q53 (Case Study): Grievance Escalation Path
    Question: Describes escalation path as "AMC grievance cell -> If unresolved
    within 30 days, file on SCORES -> ODR"

    ISSUE: The PDF (page 90) states grievances must be redressed "not later than
    twenty-one calendar days from the date of receipt" -- NOT 30 days. The Q53
    answer says "within 30 days" which is incorrect per the PDF.

    RECOMMENDATION: Change "within 30 days" to "within 21 calendar days" in
    the correct answer option to match the PDF.

11. Scheme Related Q22: Rights of Unitholders Location
    Question: "The rights of unitholders in a mutual fund scheme are detailed in:"
    Current Correct Answer: "Scheme Information Document (SID)"
    Marked Wrong: "Statement of Additional Information (SAI)"

    ISSUE: Both the SID and SAI contain sections on rights of unitholders. The PDF
    confirms:
    - SID table of contents includes "Rights of Unit-holders" (page 96)
    - SAI content table explicitly says "Rights of Unit-holders - SAI details
      the rights to beneficial ownership..." (page 102)
    Marking SAI as wrong is misleading since the SAI also contains unitholder rights.

    RECOMMENDATION: Reword question to "Which document contains the MOST
    DETAILED description of scheme-specific unitholder rights?" or acknowledge both
    SID and SAI contain rights information. The SAI option should not be presented
    as flatly wrong.

12. Fund Distribution Q25: India Post Not a Recognized Channel
    Question: "Which of the following is NOT a recognized transaction channel for
    mutual fund investments in India?"
    Current Correct Answer: "India Post offices as authorized mutual fund
    transaction points"

    ISSUE: India Post has historically been involved in mutual fund distribution
    through post offices. While it may not be a major channel, some AMCs have
    used post offices. The PDF (Chapter 6) does not mention post offices as a
    distribution channel, so the answer is defensible in context. However, India
    Post Payments Bank (IPPB) does distribute mutual funds.

    ASSESSMENT: Borderline. The answer is defensible within the scope of the
    workbook but could be challenged. Consider replacing with a clearly
    non-existent channel option.

13. Scheme Related Q30: Benchmark Change as Fundamental Attribute
    Question: "An AMC decides to change the benchmark index... This change:"
    Current Correct Answer: "May be considered a change in fundamental attributes"

    ISSUE: Per the PDF (page 101-102), fundamental attributes include:
    (i) Type of scheme, (ii) Investment Objective (including asset allocation),
    (iii) Terms of Issue (liquidity, fees, safety net).
    A benchmark change is NOT explicitly listed as a fundamental attribute.
    The answer uses "May be considered" which is appropriately hedged, but the
    PDF does not support this classification.

    ASSESSMENT: The hedged language "May be considered" makes this defensible
    but imprecise. A benchmark change would more likely be handled as a non-
    fundamental change requiring an addendum. Consider reframing.

SECTION C: CONCEPT GAPS
(Important PDF concepts not covered by any question)
================================================================================

1. SEBI GUIDELINES ON UNAUTHENTICATED NEWS CIRCULATION
   PDF Section 4.2.4 (pages 87-88) covers detailed SEBI guidelines on circulation
   of unauthenticated news through blogs, chat forums, messenger sites, etc.
   This includes requirements for compliance officers to approve market-related
   news before forwarding. No question tests this topic.

2. CYBER SECURITY AND CYBER RESILIENCE FRAMEWORK (CSCRF)
   PDF Section 4.4 (page 91-92) describes SEBI's CSCRF framework covering five
   cyber resiliency goals: Anticipate, Withstand, Contain, Recover, Evolve.
   No question covers this framework.

3. SPECIALIZED INVESTMENT FUNDS (SIF) BRANDING REQUIREMENTS
   PDF pages 87-88 describe mandatory distinct branding for SIFs (separate brand
   name, logo, website from regular MF). SIF can use sponsor brand for only 5 years.
   No question tests SIF branding requirements.

4. SIF RISK-BAND (vs. RISK-O-METER)
   PDF pages 110-111 describe the 5-level Risk-Band system for SIFs, which is
   different from the 6-level Risk-o-meter for regular MFs. No question covers this
   distinction.

5. PORTFOLIO DISCLOSURE FREQUENCY DIFFERENCES
   PDF page 109-110 distinguishes:
   - Regular MF schemes: monthly (within 10 days)
   - Debt schemes: fortnightly (within 5 days)
   - SIFs: every alternate month
   No question tests these different frequencies.

6. CHANGE OF DISTRIBUTOR CODE - NO COMMISSION RULE
   PDF Section 6.10 (pages 133-134) has an important rule: when an investor
   changes distributor code, NO commission is payable to either old or new
   distributor. Exception: if change is due to distributor's voluntary cessation.
   No question tests this specific rule.

7. DISTRIBUTOR SELF-BUSINESS - NO COMMISSION
   PDF page 127 states: "no commission is payable to the distributor for their
   own investments (self-business)." No question specifically tests this rule.

8. NOMINATION FACILITY FOR DISTRIBUTORS
   PDF Section 6.9 (pages 132-133) covers nomination facility for MFDs including:
   - Commission payment to nominees/legal heirs
   - Nominee need not be ARN holder
   - No new business under deceased MFD's ARN
   - Transfer of AUM requires valid ARN for nominee
   Only partially tested through general questions.

9. INVESTMENT IN UNLISTED DEBT INSTRUMENTS
   PDF page 82 covers unlisted debt investment limits (max 10% of debt portfolio
   for NCDs) and related restrictions. No question specifically tests this.

10. PARKING OF FUNDS IN SHORT-TERM DEPOSITS
    PDF page 82 covers the 15%/20% limits for parking funds in scheduled
    commercial bank deposits and the restriction that the scheme cannot invest
    in deposits of a bank that has invested in the scheme. No question tests this.

11. OPEN-ENDED DEBT FUND LIQUIDITY REQUIREMENT
    PDF page 82 states open-ended debt funds must maintain minimum 10% of
    corpus in liquid assets (cash, G-Secs, T-bills, Repo on G-Secs). Not
    applicable to liquid/overnight funds. No question tests this.

12. INVESTMENT RESTRICTIONS FOR InvITs
    PDF page 83 covers specific limits: no MF shall own >10% of InvIT units;
    scheme max 10% NAV in InvITs; max 5% in single issuer. No question tests these.

13. CELEBRITY ENDORSEMENT RULES - PRIOR SEBI APPROVAL
    PDF page 87 states prior approval of SEBI is required for celebrity endorsement
    at industry level. Questions mention celebrity endorsements are permitted at
    industry level but don't test the prior SEBI approval requirement.

14. UNCLAIMED AMOUNTS TREATMENT
    PDF Section 4.2.5 (pages 89-90) distinguishes:
    - Within 3 years: payment at prevailing NAV
    - After 3 years: payment at NAV as of end of 3 years
    No question tests this time-based distinction.

15. PROCEEDS OF ILLIQUID SECURITIES
    PDF pages 90-91 covers treatment of excess recovery:
    - Substantial amount recovered within 2 years: paid to old investors
    - Other cases: transferred to Investor Education Fund
    No question tests this.

16. GST APPLICABILITY ON DISTRIBUTOR COMMISSION
    PDF Section 6.5.4 (page 128) covers GST reverse charge mechanism for
    unregistered distributors. Only briefly mentioned in questions, not directly
    tested.

17. EXECUTION ONLY PLATFORMS (EOPs)
    PDF page 81 mentions SEBI guidelines for EOPs but no question specifically
    tests EOP regulations, categories, or eligibility criteria.

18. AMFI CODE OF CONDUCT - BREACH SEQUENCE
    PDF page 93 describes the specific sequence:
    Step 1: Write to intermediary, ask explanation within 3 weeks
    Step 2: Warning letter if no/unsatisfactory explanation
    Step 3: Second proven violation = cancellation of registration
    No question tests this specific escalation sequence.

19. ADDITIONAL TER FOR EXIT-LOAD-FREE SCHEMES
    PDF page 147 states that schemes where exit load is not levied/applicable
    cannot charge the additional 0.05% expense. No question tests this
    restriction.

20. COMMODITY RISK SCORING FOR RISK-O-METER
    PDF page 108-109 provides a specific table for commodity risk scoring based
    on annualized volatility (<10% = Moderate, 10-15% = Moderately High,
    15-20% = High, >20% = Very High). No question tests this.
