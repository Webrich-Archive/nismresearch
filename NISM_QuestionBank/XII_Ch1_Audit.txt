ISSUE 1 (MEDIUM SEVERITY) - Question 6
File: Understanding Securities Markets and Performance_6.xml
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question: "The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the regulator of which of the
following market segments?"
Current correct answer: "Money market and government securities market"

ISSUE: The PDF (Section 1.3, page 25) states: "The Reserve Bank of India
regulates the money market and FOREIGN EXCHANGE market segments." The
government securities market role is described separately: "As the manager of
the government's borrowing programs, RBI is the issue manager for the
government. It controls and regulates the government securities market."

The correct answer omits "foreign exchange market" (which is explicitly named
as a primary regulatory segment) and substitutes "government securities market"
instead. While RBI does regulate government securities, the PDF's primary
formulation pairs money market with FOREIGN EXCHANGE market.

RECOMMENDED FIX: Change the correct answer to:
  "Money market and foreign exchange market"
OR to be fully comprehensive:
  "Money market, foreign exchange market, and government securities market"
Also update the explanation to reflect the foreign exchange role.

Alternative: If the answer is kept as-is, at minimum add "foreign exchange
market" to the explanation so students are not misled about RBI's forex role.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE 2 (LOW SEVERITY) - Question 23
File: Understanding Securities Markets and Performance_23.xml
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question: "A COMPANY issues a Certificate of Deposit (CD) for 1 year..."

ISSUE: The PDF (Section 1.2.1, page 17) describes the CD example as:
"Assume instead that, THE BANK issues a certificate of deposit (CD)..."

In the PDF, it is a BANK that issues the CD, not a "company." CDs are
typically issued by banks and financial institutions. The question text should
say "A bank" instead of "A company."

RECOMMENDED FIX: Change the question stem from:
  "A company issues a Certificate of Deposit (CD) for 1 year..."
to:
  "A bank issues a Certificate of Deposit (CD) for 1 year..."

This is a factual accuracy issue -- CDs are bank instruments, and the PDF
example explicitly uses "bank."

SECTION B: CONFIRMED FINE
========================================

The following questions have been verified against the PDF text and are
factually accurate with correctly marked answers:

Q1  (Primary function of securities market) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 14-15, Section 1.1. "regulated institutional framework for
    an efficient flow of capital (equity and debt) from investors to
    businesses" matches the correct answer verbatim.

Q2  (What is NOT a security under SCRA) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 15-17, Section 1.2. Bank fixed deposits are explicitly
    listed as NOT securities on page 17: "Bank deposits, inter-corporate
    deposits, company fixed deposits...are all not securities."

Q3  (Primary market definition) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 18, Section 1.3. "The primary market, also called the new
    issue market, is where issuers raise capital by issuing securities to
    investors."

Q4  (Institutional investors) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Pages 18-19, Section 1.3. "Institutional investors are
    organizations that invest large sums of money and employ specialized
    knowledge and investment skills." Examples include mutual funds, insurance
    companies, pension trusts.

Q5  (SEBI as chief regulator) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 24, Section 1.3. "SEBI...is the chief regulator of
    securities markets in India." Established under SEBI Act, 1992.

Q7  (PFRDA role) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 26, Section 1.3. PFRDA established under PFRDA Act, 2013
    "to promote old age income security by establishing, developing and
    regulating pension funds."

Q8  (Not a SEBI intermediary) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Pages 20-23, Section 1.3. The SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations,
    2008 list does not include commercial banks in their lending capacity.
    Banks appear only as "Bankers to an issue" -- a specific issuance role.

Q9  (Dematerialisation) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 20, Section 1.3. "Previously issued share certificates also
    were converted into electronic form by the issuers. This process is called
    dematerialisation."

Q10 (RTAs maintain investor records) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 22, Section 1.3. "Registrars & Share Transfer Agents
    maintain the records of investors for the issuer" including holder details,
    addresses, bank accounts, signatures, nominations.

Q11 (Price discovery) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 28, Section 1.4. "prices are determined by the actions of
    millions of buyers and sellers...This process is known as 'price
    discovery'."

Q12 (Liquidity role) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 27, Section 1.4. "Secondary markets provide liquidity to
    securities, by allowing them to be sold and converted to cash."

Q13 (DPs as agents of depositories) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 22, Section 1.3. "DPs act as agents of the Depositories."
    Verbatim match.

Q14 (Code of conduct for intermediaries) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 23, Section 1.3. Key points include "protection of investor
    interests, providing fair, professional and skilled services, avoiding
    collusion..."

Q15 (IFSCA role) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Pages 26-27, Section 1.3. IFSCA headquartered at GIFT City,
    established in 2020 under IFSCA Act, 2019, as "unified authority for the
    development and regulation of financial products, financial services and
    financial institutions in the IFSC."

Q16 (Credit rating agencies) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 22, Section 1.3. "Credit rating agencies evaluate a debt
    security to provide a professional opinion about the ability of the issuer
    to meet the obligations for payment of interest and return of principal."

Q17 (Financial disintermediation) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 27, Section 1.4. "The expansion of securities markets has
    contributed to the process of financial disintermediation. For example, a
    company may find it more cost-efficient to raise funds by issuing
    commercial paper or bonds rather than obtaining a traditional line of
    credit or term loan from the bank."

Q18 (Five CSCRF cyber resiliency goals) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Pages 28-29, Section 1.5.1. Five goals listed: Anticipate,
    Withstand, Contain, Recover, Evolve.

Q19 (Regulatory sandbox - no KYC/AML relaxation) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 33, Section 1.5.2. "no exemption will be granted from the
    investor protection, Know-Your-Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering
    (AML) rules."

Q20 (Merchant banker functions) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 21, Section 1.3. "evaluate the capital needs, structure an
    appropriate instrument, get involved in pricing the instrument, and manage
    the entire issue process until the securities are issued and listed."

Q21 (Information signaling through prices) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 28, Section 1.4. "Information about the issuer of the
    security or its assets is reflected quickly in secondary market prices."

Q22 (KRA centralized KYC system) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 23, Section 1.3. "KRA provides a centralised system wherein
    the KYC information are uploaded by the securities markets intermediaries."
    "KYC once completed, is valid across all SEBI-registered intermediaries."

Q24 (Fertilizer company and monsoon - info signaling) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 28, Section 1.4. Uses the exact same example from the PDF:
    "a poor monsoon has a negative impact on agricultural inputs such as
    fertilizers and seeds, so prices of securities issued by fertilizer
    manufacturing companies may go down."

Q25 (100 shares profit calculation + liquidity) - CONFIRMED CORRECT
    PDF ref: Page 27, Section 1.4. Uses the exact same example: "100 shares
    of XYZ Ltd. at Rs.25 per share...price of the share is quoting at
    Rs.45...earn Rs.20 per share, or a total of Rs.2000." Correctly
    attributes to liquidity function.

SECTION C: CONCEPT GAPS
========================================
The following important concepts from Chapter 1 are NOT covered by any of
the 25 questions. Consider adding questions on these topics:

GAP 1: SECURITIES CONTRACTS (REGULATION) ACT - SECTION REFERENCE
  The PDF specifies "Section 2(h)" of the SCRA, 1956 for the definition of
  securities. No question tests the specific section reference. This is a
  common exam-style question (e.g., "The definition of securities is found
  in which section of which Act?").

GAP 2: SPECIFIC ITEMS IN THE DEFINITION OF SECURITIES
  The PDF lists items (a) through (j) in the definition of securities,
  including newer additions like Electronic Gold Receipts (Gazette
  notification Dec 24, 2021) and Zero Coupon Zero Principal Instruments
  (Gazette notification July 16, 2022, issued by not-for-profit
  organizations registered with Social Stock Exchanges). No question tests
  these specific recent additions.

GAP 3: INSURANCE POLICIES AND PROVIDENT FUNDS AS NON-SECURITIES
  While Q2 tests bank FDs as non-securities, the PDF also explicitly states:
  "Insurance policies are also contracts and are not securities. Investment
  in provident funds or pension funds is also not investment in securities."
  A question testing whether insurance policies or PF investments are
  securities would be valuable.

GAP 4: PRIMARY DEALERS
  The PDF mentions: "specialist intermediaries performing underwriting
  activities in the government bond market are called primary dealers." No
  question covers this concept.

GAP 5: AUTHORIZED PERSONS (APs)
  The PDF describes: "Authorized persons (AP) are agents of the brokers
  (previously referred to as sub-brokers) and are registered with the
  respective stock exchanges." No question tests this concept or the
  sub-broker to AP transition.

GAP 6: CUSTODIANS
  While custodians are mentioned as a distractor in some questions, no
  question specifically tests the custodian's role: "typically work with
  institutional investors, holding securities and bank accounts on their
  behalf."

GAP 7: TRUSTEES (MUTUAL FUND AND DEBENTURE)
  The PDF describes: "Mutual fund trustees are appointed to supervise the
  asset managers; debenture trustees are appointed to ensure that the
  lenders interests are protected." No question covers this.

GAP 8: INVESTMENT ADVISERS
  The PDF describes investment advisers' role in helping investors choose
  securities and creating financial plans. No question tests this.

GAP 9: MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS (MCA) AND COMPANIES ACT
  The PDF states MCA regulates corporate functioning and the Companies Act
  governs issuance of securities. While MCA appears as a distractor in Q5,
  no question specifically tests MCA's role.

GAP 10: INNOVATION SANDBOX vs REGULATORY SANDBOX DISTINCTION
  The PDF describes TWO sandboxes: (a) Innovation Sandbox for offline
  testing by FinTech firms in isolation from the live market, and (b)
  Regulatory Sandbox for live testing with limited customers. Q19 tests
  the Regulatory Sandbox, but no question tests the Innovation Sandbox or
  the distinction between the two.

GAP 11: CSCRF - MARKET INFRASTRUCTURE INSTITUTIONS (MIIs) LIST
  The PDF lists 5 entities as MIIs under CSCRF: Stock Exchanges,
  Depositories, Clearing Corporations, KRAs, and QRTAs. No question tests
  this specific list (which notably includes KRAs and QRTAs beyond the
  traditional three MIIs).

GAP 12: SEBI'S SPECIFIC POWERS
  The PDF details SEBI's powers: "call for information, summon persons for
  interrogation, examine witnesses and conduct search and seizure" and
  penalties including "suspension, monetary penalties and prosecution." No
  question tests these specific enforcement powers.

GAP 13: INSIDER TRADING REGULATIONS
  The PDF covers SEBI's insider trading regulations including requirements
  for a compliance officer, periodic disclosure of holdings, data
  confidentiality, and penalties (monetary, criminal prosecution, banning
  from markets, voiding transactions). No question covers this.

GAP 14: SEBI (ICDR) REGULATIONS, 2018
  The PDF references "SEBI (Issuance of Capital and Disclosures
  Requirements) Regulations, 2018" governing public issues. No question
  tests this regulation name or its scope.

GAP 15: TYPES OF ISSUERS
  The PDF lists 7 types of issuers: Companies, Central/State Governments,
  Local governments/municipalities, Financial institutions/Banks, Public
  Sector Companies (disinvestment), Mutual Funds (units), and Investment
  Trusts (REITs, InvITs, AIFs). No question tests this classification
  or asks which entities can/cannot issue equity (e.g., "Government
  agencies do not issue equity securities").

GAP 16: DIGITAL KYC FOR PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES
  The PDF mentions a May 2025 SEBI circular requiring that "registered
  intermediaries ensure digital KYC process are accessible and inclusive
  for person with disabilities, inclusive those with visual impairments."
  This is a recent update that could appear on the exam.

GAP 17: AADHAAR-BASED KYC VALIDATION
  The PDF details KRA validation through (a) UIDAI for Aadhaar, (b) OTP
  for mobile/email, and (c) Income Tax database for PAN. No question
  tests this validation mechanism.
