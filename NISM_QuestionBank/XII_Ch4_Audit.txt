1. Secondary Markets_41.xml - Circuit Breaker: Distractor (c) is ALSO correct
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE: The question asks "What is the market-wide circuit breaker limit that
triggers trading halt for the rest of the day?" The correct answer is given as
"20 percent decline in the index at any time during market hours."

However, distractor (c) "15 percent decline in the index after 2 PM" is ALSO
correct per the PDF. Table 4.1 (page 133) clearly states that a 15% decline
"On or after 2 p.m." triggers a halt for "Rest of the Day."

FIX: Either (a) reword the question to ask "What percentage decline ALWAYS
triggers a rest-of-day halt regardless of the time of occurrence?" to make
only 20% correct, or (b) change distractor (c) to something clearly wrong like
"15 percent decline in the index before 1 PM" (which only causes a 1h 45min
halt, not rest-of-day).

RECOMMENDED REWRITE of distractor (c):
  "15 percent decline in the index before 1 PM"

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Secondary Markets_50.xml - Trading Cost Calculation: Answer Includes DP
   Charges Not Stated in the Question
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE: The question states specific components: brokerage 0.3%, STT 0.1%,
GST 18% on brokerage, stamp duty 0.015%, SEBI turnover fee 0.0001%.
Calculating these: 300 + 100 + 54 + 15 + 0.10 = Rs. 469.10.

The correct answer is stated as "Rs. 489" which matches the PDF workbook
illustration (page 138) that also includes DP charges of Rs. 20 (0.02%).
However, the QUESTION TEXT does not mention DP charges as a component.

The explanation acknowledges this by saying "Adding DP charges of approximately
Rs. 20, total is about Rs. 489" -- but DP charges are not listed in the
question stem.

FIX: Either (a) add "DP charges at 0.02%" to the question stem to make the
Rs. 489 answer fully derivable from the stated inputs, or (b) change the
correct answer to "Rs. 469" (matching only the stated components). Option (a)
is preferred since it matches the PDF workbook illustration exactly.

RECOMMENDED REWRITE of question text:
  Add ", and DP charges at 0.02%" before "What is the approximate total cost"

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Secondary Markets_15.xml - Pre-Open Session Timing: Explanation Slightly
   Misleading
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE: The explanation says "9:00-9:15 AM" for the pre-open session. The PDF
(page 128) states the pre-open session is "generally 15 minutes before trading
starts for the day" and trading starts at 9:15 AM, which would place it at
9:00-9:15 AM. This is consistent. However, the question answer says "A
15-minute session before regular trading begins" which is correct.

The explanation breakdown says "7-8 min order entry -> 4 min matching -> 3 min
buffer -> 9:15 AM regular trading starts." The PDF says the order entry period
is "randomly stopped between the 7th and 8th minute" -- the explanation is
accurate here.

FIX: No fix needed. CONFIRMED FINE.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Secondary Markets_18.xml - STT Explanation: Needs Nuance
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE: The explanation says "For delivery-based equity trades, both buyer and
seller pay 0.100% each." This is correct per the PDF table on page 135.
However, the explanation also says "For intra-day (non-delivery), the seller
pays 0.025%." The PDF (page 135, row 4) confirms the 0.025% rate for sale of
equity settled "otherwise than by actual delivery."

The explanation also says "Options sale = 0.1% on premium" which matches PDF
page 136 ("Sale of an option in securities: 0.1 percent, Seller - on the
option premium").

FIX: No fix needed. All figures verified correct. CONFIRMED FINE.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Secondary Markets_47.xml - Cross-Margining: Spread Margin Percentage
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE: The explanation states "only a spread margin of 25% of applicable
upfront margin is charged on offsetting positions." The PDF (page 142) confirms
"the exchange, therefore, charges a spread margin equal to 25 per cent of the
applicable upfront margin on the positions in the cash and futures segment that
offset each other."

HOWEVER, the PDF (page 143) also states a DIFFERENT rate for highly
co-related equity indices: "stock exchanges are to charge a spread margin of
30 percent of the total applicable margin on the eligible off-setting
positions."

FIX: The question and answer are correct as stated (25% for cash-futures
offsetting). Consider adding a note in the explanation that the 30% rate
applies to highly co-related equity indices specifically. LOW PRIORITY.
