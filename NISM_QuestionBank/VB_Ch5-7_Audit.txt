    months from the end of the 1st half or 2nd half of the financial year in
    which schemes were launched. Subsequently, SID shall be updated within ONE
    MONTH from the end of each half-year (based on data as at end of September

    PROBLEM: The PDF indicates SID updates are effectively TWICE a year (each

    year" is technically not wrong (twice a year satisfies "at least once") but
    is misleading and imprecise. The PDF clearly specifies a half-yearly update
    cycle.
    FIX: Change correct answer to "At least once every half-year (within one
    month from end of each half-year)" or similar. Update explanation to
    reference the half-yearly update requirement from Section 5.1.5.

1.7 VB Performance of Mutual Funds - Missing Sharpe/Treynor/IR content
    ISSUE: No questions on Sharpe Ratio, Treynor Ratio, Information Ratio,
    or Tracking Error despite significant PDF coverage
    PDF COVERAGE (Section 7.4, pp.110-112): The PDF devotes substantial
    content to:
    - Sharpe Ratio formula and example (Sharpe = (Rs - Rf) / Std Dev)
    - Treynor Ratio formula and example (Treynor = (Rs - Rf) / Beta)
    - Information Ratio (mandatory disclosure per SEBI circular Jan 2025)
    - Tracking Error for index funds
    CURRENT QUESTIONS: The 30 Performance questions cover NAV, absolute
    return, CAGR, standard deviation, benchmarks, and general concepts but
    have ZERO questions on these four risk-adjusted return measures.
    FIX: This is a significant gap. Add questions on:
    (a) Sharpe Ratio formula and interpretation (higher = better)
    (b) Treynor Ratio formula and when to use it (diversified equity)
    (c) Information Ratio - the only mandatory risk-adjusted measure to
        disclose per SEBI
    (d) Tracking Error - especially for index funds
    (e) Numerical calculation questions for Sharpe and Treynor
    These are testable concepts per the PDF's sample questions (Ch7 Q1 asks
    about risk-adjusted returns with Sharpe Ratio as correct answer).

*** HIGH PRIORITY GAPS ***

GAP 1: SHARPE RATIO (Chapter 7, Section 7.4)
  The PDF provides a detailed formula, example (risk-free 5%, return 7%,
  std dev 0.5% = Sharpe Ratio of 4), and states higher is better.
  The PDF's own sample question Q1 tests Sharpe Ratio.
  NO QUESTION covers this in the bank.
  RECOMMENDED: Add 2-3 questions including formula and calculation.

GAP 2: TREYNOR RATIO (Chapter 7, Section 7.4)
  Formula: (Rs - Rf) / Beta. Example given. Best for diversified equity.
  PDF states higher is better.
  NO QUESTION covers this.
  RECOMMENDED: Add 1-2 questions.

GAP 3: INFORMATION RATIO (Chapter 7, Section 7.4)
  The PDF states this is the ONLY mandatory risk-adjusted return measure
  per SEBI (circular dated January 17, 2025). Must be disclosed daily on
  AMC website and AMFI website.
  Formula: (Portfolio Return - Benchmark Return) / Std Dev of Excess Return
  NO QUESTION covers this.
  RECOMMENDED: Add 2 questions - one on the formula, one on the mandatory
  disclosure requirement (this is a very testable regulatory fact).

GAP 4: TRACKING ERROR (Chapter 7, Section 7.4)
  Standard deviation of excess returns. Critical for index funds (should be
  very low). PDF provides clear explanation.
  NO QUESTION covers this.
  RECOMMENDED: Add 1-2 questions.

GAP 5: BETA CONCEPT (Chapter 7, Section 7.4)
  Beta is mentioned in Treynor Ratio context and as market risk measure.
  Beta of market = 1 by definition. Used in Treynor Ratio.
  NO QUESTION covers Beta specifically.
  RECOMMENDED: Add 1 question.

GAP 6: EXIT LOAD IMPACT ON RETURNS (Chapter 7, Section 7.2)

GAP 8: MIS-SELLING DEFINITION (Chapter 6, Box 6.8.3)

GAP 15: NAV DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS (Chapter 5, Section 5.1.6)

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
The existing 85 questions are broadly accurate in their factual content.
The correct answers align with the PDF in the vast majority of cases. The
main concerns are:

1. CRITICAL: The Performance of Mutual Funds section (30 questions) has a
   major gap in risk-adjusted return measures (Sharpe, Treynor, Information
   Ratio, Tracking Error). These are covered extensively in the PDF and
   appear in the PDF's own sample questions but are entirely absent from
   the question bank.

2. IMPORTANT: The Fund Distribution section (25 questions) lacks questions
   on commission disclosure thresholds, change of distributor rules,
   mis-selling definitions, EUIN, and KYD process.

3. MODERATE: Several questions under "Scheme Related Information" actually
   test Chapter 7 (Performance) concepts (benchmarks, TRI).

4. MINOR: A few terminology differences between question answers and the
   exact PDF wording (CAGR expansion, SID update frequency).

The question bank would benefit most from adding 15-20 questions covering
the high-priority gaps, particularly the risk-adjusted return measures
and the regulatory-specific numerical thresholds.
