### Caselet Preparation Tips
- Caselets test **application of concepts**, not just recall
- Read the entire scenario before answering any question
- Time allocation: spend ~50% more time per caselet mark than per independent MCQ mark
- Common caselet themes: financial planning scenarios (X-A/X-B), company valuation (XV), fund structuring (XIX series), portfolio construction (XXI-B)

## Detailed Exam-by-Exam Analysis

### Series I: Currency Derivatives
- **Question Mix:** ~25% calculation, ~45% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Currency futures pricing (cost of carry), options payoff, cross-rate calculations, P&L on hedged positions, margin calculations
- **Sample Calculation Q:** "If USD/INR spot is 83.50 and 3-month forward is 84.20, what is the annualized forward premium?"
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is the role of derivatives?"
- **Prep Tip:** Focus on Chapters 3-5 (futures, options, strategies) which carry ~54% weightage. Practice cross-rate and forward premium calculations.

### Series II-A: RTA Corporate
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~60% conceptual, ~25% regulatory, ~10% procedural
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - some bonus/split ratio calculations, dividend computation
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is a hybrid security?"
- **Prep Tip:** Focus on IPO processes, corporate actions, and SEBI LODR provisions. Mostly memory-based.

### Series II-B: RTA Mutual Fund
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~60% conceptual, ~25% regulatory, ~10% procedural
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - NAV computation, entry/exit load impact
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is NOT an example of corporate actions?"
- **Prep Tip:** MF operational concepts (Ch 11) and investor services carry highest weightage. Know transaction types thoroughly.

### Series III-A: Compliance (Non-Fund)
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~35% conceptual, ~55% regulatory
- **Calculation Topics:** Almost none - possibly some penalty amount questions
- **Sample Regulatory Q:** "Unhealthy practice in the Securities Markets includes which of the following?"
- **Prep Tip:** This is a regulation-heavy exam. Memorize key provisions of SEBI Act, PIT Regulations, FUTP, and PMLA. Part A (60%) is common; Part B (40%) is intermediary-specific.

### Series III-C: Compliance (Fund)
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~30% conceptual, ~60% regulatory
- **Calculation Topics:** Almost none
- **Sample Regulatory Q:** "Under FEMA, 'Import of goods of an entity' qualifies under which type of transaction?"
- **Prep Tip:** Similar to III-A but fund-focused. Part B covers MF regulations, AIF regulations, InvITs, REITs. Know FEMA provisions for fund compliance.

### Series IV: Interest Rate Derivatives
- **Question Mix:** ~35% calculation, ~35% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Bond pricing (YTM, clean/dirty price), duration & convexity, PVBP, interest rate futures pricing, options on bonds, hedging strategies
- **Sample Calculation Q:** "Calculate the modified duration of a bond with Macaulay duration of 5.2 years and YTM of 8%"
- **Prep Tip:** Hardest calculation content of any single-segment derivatives exam. Master bond math formulas. Chapters 1 and 3-5 carry 70% weightage.

### Series V-A: Mutual Fund Distributors
- **Question Mix:** ~15% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~15% regulatory, ~15% application
- **Calculation Topics:** NAV computation, expense ratio impact, CAGR, absolute return, Sharpe ratio, tax on MF gains (STCG/LTCG)
- **Sample Q:** "The NAV of scheme is disclosed on the website of ______?"
- **Prep Tip:** No negative marking, 50% passing - attempt everything. Investor Services (Ch 9, 15%) and Scheme Selection (Ch 12, 15%) are highest-weightage chapters.

### Series V-B: Mutual Fund Foundation
- **Question Mix:** ~10% calculation, ~60% conceptual, ~15% regulatory, ~15% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Basic NAV, simple return calculations
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following would be close-ended mutual fund scheme?"
- **Prep Tip:** Easiest MF exam (50 questions, 2 hours = 144 sec/question). No negative marking. Focus on investor services (20% weightage).

### Series VI: Depository Operations
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~30% regulatory, ~10% procedural
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - some settlement cycle calculations
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is NOT directly a part of the Primary Market?"
- **Prep Tip:** Heavy on DP functions (Chapters 4-11 = 81% weightage). Know account opening, demat, trading & settlement, pledge processes.

### Series VII: Securities Operations & Risk Management
- **Question Mix:** ~10% calculation, ~50% conceptual, ~25% regulatory, ~15% procedural
- **Calculation Topics:** Margin calculations, capital adequacy, settlement obligations
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following best describes the term 'Private Placement'?"
- **Prep Tip:** Broking Operations (Ch 3, 20%) is the single biggest chapter. Understand front/middle/back office functions and trade life cycle.

### Series VIII: Equity Derivatives
- **Question Mix:** ~30% calculation, ~40% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Futures fair value (cost of carry model), options payoff, Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega), margin calculation, strategy P&L (spreads, straddles, strangles, butterflies)
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is a hedged position?"
- **Prep Tip:** Chapters 3 (Futures, 25%) and 4 (Options, 25%) together carry 50% weightage. Practice payoff diagram calculations and strategy P&L tables.

### Series IX: Merchant Banking
- **Question Mix:** ~10% calculation, ~45% conceptual, ~35% regulatory, ~10% procedural
- **Calculation Topics:** IPO pricing (book building), underwriting commission, allotment calculations
- **Sample Q:** "Who is a Qualified Institutional Buyer?"
- **Prep Tip:** Issue Management chapters (4-6) carry 38% combined weightage. Know SEBI ICDR and SAST regulations thoroughly.

### Series X-A: Investment Adviser Level 1
- **Question Mix:** ~25% calculation, ~25% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 9 caselets contributing 60 marks out of 150 (40%)
- **Calculation Topics:** TVM (PV, FV, annuities), retirement corpus calculation, real vs nominal returns, portfolio return/risk, Sharpe/Treynor/Jensen ratios
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is not a part of the financial planning process?"
- **Prep Tip:** 3-hour exam with caselets - allocate 90 min for MCQs, 90 min for caselets. Module 1 (Financial Planning, 37 marks) is the heaviest. TVM concepts recur across multiple caselets.

### Series X-B: Investment Adviser Level 2
- **Question Mix:** ~30% calculation, ~20% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 6 caselets with 5 Qs each at 2 marks = 60 marks (40%)
- **Calculation Topics:** Insurance premium calculations, retirement accumulation/decumulation math, all heads of income tax computation, capital gains with indexation, DTAA relief, real estate valuation, risk profiling scores
- **Sample Calculation Q:** "On retirement, how much will Mr. Z have in his retirement corpus?"
- **Prep Tip:** Taxation modules (VII-XI) are the most calculation-heavy. Master capital gains computation with indexation. Caselets are typically financial planning scenarios.

### Series X-C: Investment Adviser Renewal
- **Question Mix:** ~10% calculation, ~60% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% updates
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - refresher level
- **Prep Tip:** Online exam, no negative marking, 50% passing. Complete the 3-hour pre-recorded training first. Tests awareness of regulatory updates since last certification.

### Series XII: Securities Markets Foundation
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~65% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - basic NAV, simple P/E ratio
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is the primary function of the secondary markets?"
- **Prep Tip:** Entry-level exam, no negative marking. Equal 20% weightage across Chapters 2-5. Good breadth coverage required.

### Series XIII: Common Derivatives (Composite)
- **Question Mix:** ~30% calculation, ~40% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** All calculations from Series I + IV + VIII combined: currency forward pricing, bond math (duration, convexity, PVBP), equity futures/options pricing, Greeks, strategy payoffs, margin calculations
- **Prep Tip:** 150 questions in 3 hours = 72 sec/question. The bond math (from Series IV content) is the hardest calculation area. Study material is the 3 workbooks (Series I, IV, VIII). Budget extra time for calculation questions.

### Series XV: Research Analyst
- **Question Mix:** ~25% calculation, ~35% conceptual, ~20% caselet-based, ~15% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 5 cases with 4 Qs each = 20 marks (20%). **Newly added in Jan 2026 revision.**
- **Calculation Topics:** P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, DCF valuation, DDM, FCFE/FCFF, financial ratio analysis, earnings yield, ROE/ROCE, technical analysis indicators
- **Sample Calculation Q:** "What is the earnings yield, if the price of a stock is Rs. 195 and EPS is Rs. 13?"
- **Prep Tip:** Revised syllabus (Jan 2026) adds Technical Analysis (15 marks) and case studies. Company Analysis (Ch 7-8, ~18% combined) and Valuation (Ch 10, 12%) are the most calculation-intensive.

### Series XVI: Commodity Derivatives
- **Question Mix:** ~20% calculation, ~45% conceptual, ~25% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Commodity futures pricing, basis calculations, options payoff, hedging effectiveness, P&L on hedged positions, margin requirements
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following can be used as a Hedging Instrument?"
- **Prep Tip:** Structure mirrors Series VIII but for commodities. Chapters 3-4 (futures + options, 30%) are the calculation core. Understand physical delivery settlement differences.

### Series XVII: Retirement Adviser
- **Question Mix:** ~15% calculation, ~50% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~15% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Retirement corpus calculation, NPS tier structure, annuity calculations, replacement ratio, inflation-adjusted returns
- **Prep Tip:** NPS chapter (Ch 4) carries highest weightage (20%). Know NPS architecture, investment choices, and exit rules thoroughly.

### Series XIX-A: AIF Distributors (Cat I&II)
- **Question Mix:** ~15% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** NAV computation, management fee/performance fee impact, IRR/XIRR, TVPI, DPI
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following persons is eligible to be the sponsor of an AIF?"
- **Prep Tip:** 10% negative marking (lighter than 25% in other exams). Fund Due Diligence (Ch 8, 15%) is highest-weightage chapter.

### Series XIX-B: AIF Distributors (Cat III)
- **Question Mix:** ~15% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~20% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Similar to XIX-A plus hedge fund-specific: long-short P&L, leverage ratios, derivatives overlay
- **Prep Tip:** Regulatory Framework (Ch 5, 15%) and Fee Structure/Benchmarking (Ch 6, 15%) are the highest-weightage chapters.

### Series XIX-C: AIF Managers (All Categories)
- **Question Mix:** ~25% calculation, ~25% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 6 caselets with 5 Qs each at 2 marks = 60 marks (40%)
- **Calculation Topics:** IRR, TVPI, DPI, RVPI, MOIC, J-Curve modeling, NAV with waterfall distribution, carried interest calculation, hurdle rate, DCF valuation, mark-to-market
- **Prep Tip:** The most comprehensive AIF exam (573 pages of study material, 150 marks, 3 hours). Caselets are typically fund structuring or performance evaluation scenarios. Master the AIF performance metrics.

### Series XIX-D: AIF Managers (Cat I&II)
- **Question Mix:** ~20% calculation, ~30% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 4 caselets with 5 Qs each at 2 marks = 40 marks (40%)
- **Calculation Topics:** Similar to XIX-C but focused on Cat I&II: venture capital IRR, PE fund waterfall, infrastructure fund metrics
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following investors CANNOT invest in an Angel Fund?"
- **Prep Tip:** Subset of XIX-C content. Investment Strategies (Ch 9) and Valuation (Ch 11) are where most calculations appear.

### Series XIX-E: AIF Managers (Cat III)
- **Question Mix:** ~20% calculation, ~30% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 4 caselets with 5 Qs each at 2 marks = 40 marks (40%)
- **Calculation Topics:** Hedge fund NAV, long-short portfolio construction, derivatives P&L, leverage analysis, risk-adjusted returns
- **Prep Tip:** Investment Strategies chapter (Ch 10, ~12%) focuses on hedge fund strategies - long/short equity, market neutral, event-driven, derivatives-based.

### Series XXI-A: PMS Distributors
- **Question Mix:** ~15% calculation, ~50% conceptual, ~25% regulatory, ~10% application
- **Calculation Topics:** Portfolio return calculation, Sharpe ratio, benchmark comparison, fee impact on returns
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "What is the expected return of the three stock portfolio described below?"
- **Prep Tip:** 10% negative marking. Revised March 2025. Know PMS minimum investment thresholds, fee structures, and regulatory requirements.

### Series XXI-B: Portfolio Managers
- **Question Mix:** ~25% calculation, ~15% conceptual, ~40% caselet-based, ~10% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 6 caselets with 5 Qs each at 2 marks = 60 marks (40%). **Highest caselet proportion of any NISM exam.**
- **Calculation Topics:** Modern Portfolio Theory (efficient frontier, CAPM, beta), duration/convexity for bond portfolios, equity valuation, performance attribution, TWRR vs MWRR, tracking error, information ratio
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following is a step in the portfolio management process?"
- **Prep Tip:** At 150 marks in 3 hours (72 sec/mark or 90 sec/question), time management is critical. Practice caselets extensively. Chapters 15-16 (MPT + Capital Market Theory) and Chapters 18-20 (Equity/FI strategies + Performance) are the calculation-heavy areas.

### Series XXIII: Social Impact Assessors
- **Question Mix:** ~5% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~15% caselet, ~20% regulatory
- **Caselet Structure:** 3 caselets with 5 Qs each = 15 marks (15%)
- **Calculation Topics:** Minimal - some social return on investment (SROI) calculations
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "What is the Logical Framework (Logframe) used for?"
- **Prep Tip:** Unique exam focused on social sector. Chapters 4-6 (impact assessment frameworks) carry the most weightage. Case studies are real-world social enterprise scenarios.

### Series XXIV: AML & CFT Provisions
- **Question Mix:** ~0% calculation, ~50% conceptual, ~0% caselet, ~45% regulatory
- **Calculation Topics:** None
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Which of the following activities should NOT be done by a reporting entity?"
- **Prep Tip:** Shortest and easiest exam (50 Qs, 1 hour, 50% passing, no negative marking). Pure knowledge recall of PMLA provisions, AML guidelines, and KYC norms. Attempt all questions.

### Series XXV-A: Research Services (Sales)
- **Question Mix:** ~0% calculation, ~55% conceptual, ~0% caselet, ~40% regulatory
- **Calculation Topics:** None
- **Prep Tip:** Newly launched (Feb 2026), remotely proctored. Complete course content within 5 days before exam. No negative marking, 50% passing.

### IFSCA-01: AML & CFT in IFSC
- **Question Mix:** ~0% calculation, ~50% conceptual, ~0% caselet, ~45% regulatory
- **Calculation Topics:** None
- **Sample Conceptual Q:** "Criminals practice money laundering by which of the following ways?"
- **Prep Tip:** Similar to Series XXIV but IFSC-focused. Chapter 6 (IFSCA KYC Norms) carries highest marks (10/50). Only 2-year validity (vs 3 years for other exams).

## Key Formula Areas by Exam

### Derivatives Exams (I, IV, VIII, XIII, XVI)
- Cost of carry: F = S x e^(r-q)T or F = S(1+r-d)^T
- Options payoff: max(S-K, 0) for calls, max(K-S, 0) for puts
- Greeks: Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega relationships
- Put-Call Parity: C - P = S - K.e^(-rT)
- Bond duration: D = sum(t.PV(CFt)) / Price
- Modified duration: D* = D / (1 + y/m)
- PVBP = Modified Duration x Price x 0.0001

### Investment/Advisory Exams (X-A, X-B, XVII)
- FV = PV(1+r)^n, PV = FV/(1+r)^n
- Annuity: PMT = PV x r / [1-(1+r)^-n]
- Real return = [(1+nominal)/(1+inflation)] - 1
- Retirement corpus = Annual expenses x (1+inflation)^n x annuity factor
- Sharpe Ratio = (Rp - Rf) / sigma_p
- Treynor Ratio = (Rp - Rf) / beta_p

### AIF/Portfolio Exams (XIX-C/D/E, XXI-B)
- IRR: NPV = 0 = sum(CF_t / (1+IRR)^t)
- TVPI = (Distributions + Residual Value) / Paid-In Capital
- DPI = Distributions / Paid-In Capital
- RVPI = Residual Value / Paid-In Capital
- MOIC = (Total Value) / Invested Capital
- TWRR vs MWRR calculation methodology
- Information Ratio = (Rp - Rb) / Tracking Error
