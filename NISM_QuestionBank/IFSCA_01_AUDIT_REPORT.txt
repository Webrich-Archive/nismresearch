1. IFSCA FATF Recommendations_10.xml
   STATUS: CORRECTION NEEDED (Incomplete answer)
   Q: "Which FATF-Style Regional Body is relevant to India?"
   Current correct answer: "Asia/Pacific Group on Money Laundering (APG), based in Sydney, Australia"
   ISSUE: The PDF (page 15) explicitly states India is a member of TWO FSRBs:
   APG and Eurasian Group of Combating Money Laundering and Financing of
   Terrorism (EAG). The question only offers APG as the correct answer and
   does not mention EAG at all -- not even as a distractor. While APG is the
   "best available" answer from the 4 choices, the question is misleading.
   FIX OPTIONS:
   (a) Change question to "Which of the following is a FATF-Style Regional
       Body of which India is a member?" (keeps current answer valid)
   (b) Add a fifth option: "Both APG and EAG" and make THAT the correct answer
   (c) Keep as-is but update the explanation to note that India is also a
       member of EAG, so candidates are not misled
   RECOMMENDATION: Option (a) or (c). This is already tested correctly in
   Introduction_6 which asks the complete question and gives the full answer.

2. IFSCA KYC Norms_9.xml
   STATUS: MINOR CLARIFICATION NEEDED (Accurate but potentially confusing)
   Q: "For a trust, which of the following must be identified as beneficial owners?"
   Current correct answer mentions 10% threshold for trust beneficiaries.
   ISSUE: The PDF contains TWO different thresholds for trust BO in IFSCA context:
   - Section 6.3.5(b)(i) says "beneficiaries with fifteen per cent. or more interest"
   - Section 6.3.6(d) says "beneficiaries with ten per cent or more interest"
   The PML Rules (Rule 9(3)(e)) also say 10%.
   The question correctly uses 10% (matching 6.3.6(d) and PML Rules).
   The explanation already acknowledges this difference.
   FIX: The existing explanation handles this well. No change strictly needed,
   but consider adding a note that this is per Section 6.3.6(d) parameters.

3. IFSCA AML CFT KYC Guidelines_20.xml
   STATUS: NEEDS VERIFICATION
   Q: "What does Clause 8.4 of the IFSCA (AML, CFT and KYC) Guidelines mandate
   regarding certification?"
   Correct answer: "Officers and employees of Regulated Entities dealing with
   AML/CFT matters must obtain relevant NISM-IFSCA certification"
   ISSUE: This specific clause (8.4 on certification) is not explicitly quoted
   in the PDF workbook text that was extracted. While the exam itself IS the
   NISM-IFSCA certification, the workbook does not reproduce clause 8.4
   verbatim. The question is likely accurate based on the actual IFSCA
   Guidelines document, but cannot be fully verified from the workbook alone.
   FIX: Consider adding a workbook page reference or mark as "based on IFSCA
   Guidelines source document."

HIGH PRIORITY (directly exam-relevant):

1. FATF IX Special Recommendations on Terrorist Financing (PDF pages 122-125)
   The PDF devotes 3+ pages to the IX Special Recommendations but there are
   ZERO questions testing these. Topics include:
   - SR I: Ratification of UN instruments
   - SR II: Criminalising financing of terrorism
   - SR III: Freezing and confiscating terrorist assets
   - SR IV: Reporting suspicious transactions related to terrorism
   - SR V: International cooperation
   - SR VI: Alternative Remittance
   - SR VII: Wire transfers
   - SR VIII: Non-profit organisations
   - SR IX: Cash Couriers
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 3-4 questions on these recommendations.

2. CDD Verification Timeline: 30 business days / 120 days termination
   (PDF pages 93-94)
   While Q KYC_12 covers the 30-day aspect, the critical follow-up is missing:
   - Suspend business relationship if verification uncompleted at 30 days
   - TERMINATE business relationship if uncompleted at 120 days
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 1 question on 120-day termination rule.

3. Low-risk CDD updation periodicity for IFSCA entities (PDF page 105)
   The questions test high-risk (annually) and medium-risk (3 years) but do
   NOT test low-risk (once in every 5 years) for IFSCA entities.
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 1 question on low-risk CDD updation = every 5 years.

4. Resident Indian customer CDD periodicity (different from standard)
   (PDF pages 105-106)
   For resident Indian customers with existing Financial Group relationship:
   - High risk: once every 2 years (vs. annual for standard)
   - Medium risk: once every 8 years (vs. 3 years for standard)
   - Low risk: once every 10 years (vs. 5 years for standard)
   This is a major differentiator and is NOT tested at all.
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 2 questions on these alternative periodicities.

5. FATF President and Vice-President details (PDF page 119)
   - Current President: Elisa de Anda Madrazo (Mexico), July 2024 - June 2026
   - Vice-President: Giles Thomson (UK), from July 1, 2025
   - Presidential term: 2 years (since April 2019 revised mandate)
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 1 question on FATF leadership/term details.

6. Black Market Peso Exchange (PDF pages 129-130)
   The PDF discusses this as a complex TBML technique but there is no question.
   RECOMMENDATION: Add 1 question.

7. Correspondent Banking prohibition with Shell Financial Institutions
   (PDF page 79)
   While Q_8 covers the definition, the specific prohibition rules are not
   fully tested -- including that correspondent banks must ensure respondent
   banks do not permit Shell FIs to use their accounts.
   RECOMMENDATION: Consider adding 1 question.
