1. SEBI Guidelines_5 (Chapter 6) -- BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP THRESHOLD FOR TRUSTS
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   Question: "For a company, what is the threshold of 'controlling ownership
   interest' for identifying the beneficial owner?"

   ISSUE IN EXPLANATION: The explanation states "Trusts = 10% or more interest"
   but the PDF (page 79, section 6.2.1 iv(e)) states:
   "Trust - the identification of beneficial owner(s) shall include identification
   of the author of the trust, the trustee, the beneficiaries with TEN PER CENT
   OR MORE interest in the trust, settlor, protector and any other natural person
   exercising ultimate effective control over the trust through a chain of
   control or ownership."

   The explanation correctly says 10% for trusts. However, the phrasing in the
   explanation says "Trusts = 10% or more interest" while the actual answer
   says ">10%". These are different: the PDF says "ten per cent or more" (>=10%)
   for trusts, but "more than ten per cent" (>10%) for companies and partnerships.

   FIX NEEDED: The explanation's cross-reference section says "Trusts = 10% or
   more interest" which is correct per PDF. But the main answer option says
   "More than 10%" for companies which is also correct per PDF (page 78:
   "ownership of or entitlement to more than ten per cent"). The answer is
   CORRECT. The explanation accurately distinguishes the thresholds.

   STATUS: FINE (on re-review, the answer and explanation are accurate)

SECTION 4: MISSING CONCEPTS (Important PDF Content Not Tested)

CHAPTER 5 (AML, CFT and PF Guidelines -- pages 69-73):
---------------------------------------------------------
1. MISSING: Persons carrying on designated business or profession under
   Clause (s) of sub-section (1) of Section 2 of PMLA. The workbook mentions
   sub-clause (vi) allowing Central Govt to designate additional persons by
   notification. No question tests this.

2. MISSING: The specific three powers under Section 51A of UAPA are tested
   individually but the third power -- "prevent the entry into or the transit
   through India of individuals listed in the Schedule" -- is only mentioned
   in explanations, never as a standalone tested concept.

3. MISSING: The specific legislations covered by VDA SP guidelines (PMLA,
   UAPA, WMDA) are tested, but Section 12 of WMDA's "mere carriage without
   knowledge" exemption for postal/financial service providers is not directly
   tested as a question (only mentioned in Q7's explanation).

4. MISSING: The "mere carriage without knowledge" proviso under Section 12
   WMDA -- that carriers/courier/telecom/postal/financial service providers
   are exempt if they carry without knowledge -- deserves its own question.

CHAPTER 6 (SEBI Guidelines -- pages 74-93):
---------------------------------------------------------
5. MISSING: Group-wide AML/CFT programmes. The PDF (page 75, section 6.1.1)
   discusses requirements for financial groups including sharing CDD info,
   group-level compliance, and safeguards on confidentiality of exchanged
   information. No question covers this.

6. MISSING: The concept that "financial groups shall be required to implement
   group wide programmes" with specific requirements (policies for info sharing,
   provision of customer/account/transaction info from branches, and
   confidentiality safeguards). This is a distinct testable concept.

7. MISSING: The internal audit function requirements for AML -- "shall be
   independent, adequately resourced and commensurate with the size of
   business" (page 77, section 6.1.2 vii). No question tests this.

8. MISSING: Non-profit organization definition. The PDF (page 91) defines
   NPO as "any entity or organisation, constituted for religious or charitable
   purposes referred to in clause (15) of section 2 of the Income-tax Act,
   1961, that is registered as a trust or a society under the Societies
   Registration Act, 1860 or a Company registered under section 8 of the
   Companies Act, 2013." No question tests this definition.

9. MISSING: The concept that registered intermediaries shall file STR
   "irrespective of the amount of transaction and/or the threshold limit
   envisaged for predicate offences specified in part B of Schedule of PMLA"
   if they have reasonable grounds to believe transactions involve proceeds
   of crime (page 92). While Q18 covers abandoned transactions, this broader
   "no threshold for STR" principle is not directly tested.

10. MISSING: The concept that "proceeds of crime" includes property "not only
    derived or obtained from the scheduled offence but also any property which
    may directly or indirectly be derived or obtained as a result of any
    criminal activity relatable to the scheduled offence" (page 92).

11. MISSING: Confidentiality requirement does not inhibit information sharing
    among entities in the group (page 92). This is an important exception
    to the tipping-off prohibition.

12. MISSING: The obligation to register non-profit organization clients on
    the DARPAN Portal of NITI Aayog (page 80, section 6.2.1 x). No question
    covers this specific requirement.

13. MISSING: Record retention for ongoing investigations -- "where records
    relate to on-going investigations or transactions which have been the
    subject of suspicious transaction reporting, they shall be retained until
    it is confirmed that the case has been closed" (page 90). This important
    exception to the 5-year rule is not tested.

14. MISSING: The four types of information to be maintained for transactions
    (nature, amount/currency, date, parties) per Rule 3 of PML Rules
    (page 87-88). While record-keeping is tested in Q13, the specific four
    data elements are not.

15. MISSING: Risk assessment requirements including assessment of ML/TF risks
    for new products, business practices, delivery mechanisms, and technologies
    (page 85, section 6.2.5). The obligation to do risk assessment PRIOR to
    launch of new products is important but not tested.

---------------------------------------------------------

17. MISSING: Change of name on PAN -- the provision that a marriage certificate

18. MISSING: e-PAN from CBDT as valid for KYC compliance (page 95-96).
    Not tested.

19. MISSING: Foreign language document translation requirement -- "If any
    proof of address is in a foreign language, then translation into English
    shall be required" (page 97-98). Not tested.

20. MISSING: The requirement that if correspondence and permanent address are
    different, proof for BOTH must be submitted (page 98).

21. MISSING: Third-party address as correspondence address -- the provision
    that KYC norms must also be fulfilled for the third party (page 98).
    Not tested.

22. MISSING: Beneficial ownership monitoring through half-yearly internal
    audits by stock exchanges and depositories (page 98-99). For mutual
    funds, by AMC Boards and Trustees.

23. MISSING: Digital KYC specific requirements including geo-location tagging,
    time stamping, end-to-end encryption, liveliness check against spoofing
    (page 103, section 7.2). While VIPV is tested, the broader app-level
    security requirements are not.

24. MISSING: The requirement that intermediaries carry out software and
    security audit of their KYC App periodically (page 103).

25. MISSING: SARAL account: the "positive confirmation" mechanisms for
    correspondence address verification (welcome kit acknowledgment, telephonic

26. MISSING: SARAL account: when KYC completion intimation letter is returned
    (wrong address/addressee not available), no transactions allowed and
    intimation sent to Stock Exchange and Depository (page 106).

27. MISSING: Confidentiality of client information -- "registered intermediaries
    shall keep confidential every information maintained, furnished or verified,
    save as otherwise provided under any law" (page 106, section 7.3.1).

28. MISSING: KRA audit requirements -- annual comprehensive audit by
    independent auditor, audit report to Board, Action Taken Report to SEBI
    within 3 months (page 107).

29. MISSING: The KUA-SubKUA mechanism for Aadhaar authentication is tested
    (Q15), but the specific entities listed as KUAs (BSE, NSDL, CDSL, CVL,
    NDML, NSE Data, CAMS Investor Services, CAMS, NSE) could be tested
