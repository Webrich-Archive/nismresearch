## Key Content Areas to Cover per Topic

### Topic 1: Cat III AIF Overview and Growth (40 Qs)
- Alternative vs traditional investments recap (overlap with XIX-A — avoid duplicating identical questions)
- Evolution of Category III AIFs in India specifically
- Types of AIFs and Category III positioning (hedge funds, complex trading strategies, leverage at fund level)
- Suitability of Cat III AIFs for different investor types
- Current Cat III AIF market status and AUM figures
- Comparison of Cat I, II, III categories
- Open-ended vs close-ended Cat III funds
- **Question mix:** 0% calc, 65% conceptual, 25% regulatory, 10% application

### Topic 2: Cat III AIF Ecosystem (60 Qs)
Read Ch 3 carefully (pages 38-87). Key areas:
- Hedge fund ecosystem: fund manager, prime broker, administrator, custodian, legal counsel
- Long-short equity strategies: market neutral, directional, sector-focused
- Leverage at fund level: mechanics, margin, short selling, leverage ratios
- Use of derivatives: options, futures, swaps, structured products
- PMS vs Category III AIF comparison (critical topic)
- Fund of Funds (FoF) investing in Cat III AIFs
- High water mark, hurdle rate, performance fee structures
- Risk management framework for Cat III AIFs
- Investor eligibility and minimum investment requirements
- **Question mix:** 15% calc, 50% conceptual, 20% regulatory, 15% application

### Topic 3: Cat III AIF Fund Structures (50 Qs)
Read Ch 4 (pages 88-105). Key areas:
- Trust, LLP, Company structures for Cat III AIFs
- Open-ended vs close-ended Cat III fund structures
- Series structure for unit issuance (multiple series with different NAVs)
- Role of service providers: custodian, fund administrator, prime broker, auditor, legal counsel
- Prime brokerage: securities lending, margin financing, trade execution
- Fund administrator: NAV calculation, investor reporting, regulatory filing
- Custodian vs prime broker distinction
- Offshore feeder fund structures
- **Question mix:** 5% calc, 55% conceptual, 30% regulatory, 10% application

### Topic 4: Cat III AIF Regulatory Framework (75 Qs)
Read Ch 5 carefully (pages 106-165) — the largest chapter. Key areas:
- SEBI AIF Regulations 2012 — provisions specific to Category III
- Registration requirements, conditions, and documentation
- Sponsor/Manager commitment for Cat III (5% of corpus or INR 10 crore, whichever is lower)
- Tenure: Cat III can be open-ended or close-ended
- Corpus and investor limits (INR 1 crore min, 1000 max per scheme)
- Investment conditions specific to Cat III: leverage limits, derivatives usage
- Operational, prudential, and reporting norms for Cat III
- Cat III participation in Credit Default Swaps
- General obligations and responsibilities
- Winding-up provisions
- Investor Charter
- Compliance Test Reporting (CTR) framework
- FATCA and CRS compliance
- SEBI (PIT) Regulations 2015 applicability to Cat III
- SEBI (PFUTP) Regulations 2003
- Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002
- FEMA 1999 — foreign investment in Cat III AIFs, overseas investments by Cat III
- SEBI (ICDR) Regulations 2018
- Accredited Investor framework for Cat III
- Direct Plan vs Distributor Plan
- **Question mix:** 5% calc, 40% conceptual, 45% regulatory, 10% application

### Topic 5: Cat III AIF Fees and Performance (75 Qs)
Read Ch 6 carefully (pages 166-237) — heavy on calculations. Key areas:
- Fee structure: management fee, performance fee, hurdle rate, high water mark
- Fee computation methods: with/without hurdle, with/without high water mark
- Equalization mechanisms for multi-series funds
- Crystallization periods for performance fees
- Catch-up and clawback provisions
- Fund performance metrics: NAV-based returns, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, Treynor ratio
- Information ratio, Jensen's alpha, Calmar ratio, maximum drawdown
- Risk metrics: standard deviation, beta, VaR (Value at Risk), Expected Shortfall
- TWRR vs MWRR for Cat III AIFs
- Benchmarking: absolute return benchmarks vs relative benchmarks
- Peer group comparison, index benchmarks
- SEBI benchmarking requirements for Cat III
- **Include at least 20 calculation questions** covering fee computation, Sharpe ratio, Sortino, VaR, drawdown, NAV computation
- **Question mix:** 30% calc, 35% conceptual, 15% regulatory, 20% application

### Topic 6: Cat III AIF Investment Strategies (40 Qs)
Read Ch 7 (pages 238-264). Key areas:
- Equity-market strategies: long-short equity, market neutral, equity hedge, short bias
- Global-macro strategy: top-down analysis, currency/interest rate/commodity bets
- Convertible arbitrage: mechanics, delta hedging, gamma exposure
- Event-driven strategies: merger arbitrage, distressed securities, activist investing, special situations
- Relative value strategies: statistical arbitrage, pairs trading
- Multi-strategy approach
- Due diligence process for Cat III AIFs: strategy assessment, risk management evaluation, operational DD
- **Question mix:** 20% calc, 50% conceptual, 10% regulatory, 20% application

### Topic 7: Cat III AIF Valuation (60 Qs)
Read Ch 8 (pages 265-296). Key areas:
- NAV computation for Cat III AIFs: daily/periodic NAV, accrual of expenses
- Valuation of listed securities: closing price, volume-weighted average price
- Valuation of unlisted securities: fair value estimation techniques
- Valuation of derivatives positions: mark-to-market, options valuation (Black-Scholes concepts)
- Computation of NAV for a Cat III AIF vs NAV attributable to a specific series of units
- Series-level NAV mechanics: equalization, new series issuance, different entry points
- Side pocketing provisions
- Role of third-party registered valuers
- SEBI valuation regulations for Cat III AIFs
- Frequency of valuation
- **Include at least 12 calculation questions** on NAV computation, mark-to-market, series NAV
- **Question mix:** 25% calc, 40% conceptual, 20% regulatory, 15% application

### Topic 8: Cat III AIF Taxation (50 Qs)
Read Ch 9 (pages 297-336). Key areas:
- Comparison of tax regime among Cat I, II, III AIFs (critical distinction: Cat III taxed at fund level)
- Taxation principles for trust-structured AIFs
- Tax on income from Cat III AIFs: business income, capital gains, dividend, interest
- Short-term vs long-term capital gains rates for Cat III
- Section 115UB provisions (tax on accreted income)
- GST on Cat III AIF services (management fees, performance fees)
- Taxation on distribution/redemption of units by Cat III AIFs
- Taxation on transfer of Cat III AIF units by investors
- Tax applicability to Cat III AIFs in IFSC (special provisions)
- Tax impact on performance: pre-tax vs post-tax returns
- Impact of Securities Transaction Tax (STT) on Cat III
- TDS/withholding tax provisions
- **Include at least 8 calculation questions** on tax computation scenarios
- **Question mix:** 20% calc, 40% conceptual, 30% regulatory, 10% application

### Topic 9: Cat III AIF Good Practices (25 Qs)
Read Ch 10 (pages 337-352). Key areas:
- Fund pitch book: role, significance, contents, best practices
- Distributor's role: pre-investment (investor education, suitability, KYC) and post-investment (monitoring, reporting)
- Client confidentiality and data privacy
- Code of Conduct for Cat III AIFs (SEBI Fourth Schedule)
- Importance of insurance for Cat III AIFs (D&O insurance, professional indemnity, crime/fidelity)
- Prohibited practices
- **Question mix:** 0% calc, 60% conceptual, 20% regulatory, 20% application

### Topic 10: Cat III AIF Due Diligence and Risk (25 Qs)
Cross-cutting from Ch 3 and 7. Key areas:

- Operational due diligence specific to Cat III: prime broker reliability, counterparty risk, valuation practices
- Risk assessment: leverage risk, liquidity risk, model risk, counterparty risk, short-selling risk
- Investor suitability assessment for Cat III products
- **Question mix:** 10% calc, 50% conceptual, 15% regulatory, 25% application
