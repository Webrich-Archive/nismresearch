--- ISSUE #1: PMLA_6 - Section 5 Attachment Period ---
File: Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002_6.xml
Question: Provisional attachment period under Section 5 of PMLA
Current correct answer: 180 days
PDF says (page 29): "not exceeding one hundred and eighty days"

VERDICT: The CORRECT ANSWER in the XML (180 days) IS CORRECT per the PDF.
HOWEVER, there is a problem: the question says "180 days" but the PDF literally
says "one hundred and eighty days" which IS 180 days. No correction needed for
the answer, BUT the explanation mentions this is "approximately 6 months" which
is fine as a rough approximation.

STATUS: FINE (no correction needed)

--- ISSUE #2: Scheduled Offences_3 - Part B Threshold ---
File: Scheduled Offences under PMLA_3.xml
Question: Part B monetary threshold
Current correct answer: "Customs Act, 1962, with the total value involved
being Rs. 1 crore or more"
PDF Section 2(y) on page 26: "offences specified under Part B of the Schedule
if the total value involved in such offences is one crore rupees or more"
PDF Chapter 4 narrative (page 58): "a monetary threshold of 3 million rupees
or 10 million rupees has been prescribed as may be applicable"

ANALYSIS: The statutory definition in Section 2(y) says "one crore rupees or
more." The Chapter 4 narrative mentions "3 million or 10 million" suggesting
there may be different thresholds for different Part B sub-offences in the
actual Schedule. However, Section 2(y) as quoted in the workbook says Rs. 1
crore. The question references Section 2(y)'s definition.

VERDICT: The question answer aligns with Section 2(y) as stated in the PDF.
However, the Chapter 4 narrative introduces ambiguity. The question's
explanation says "only Customs Act offences have the Rs. 1 crore threshold
under Part B" - this is POTENTIALLY MISLEADING because page 58 mentions
two different thresholds (Rs. 30 lakh and Rs. 1 crore). The question should
perhaps note this nuance.

STATUS: MINOR CONCERN - The Rs. 1 crore answer is defensible from Section 2(y),
but the explanation oversimplifies. No mandatory correction, but explanation
could be enriched.

SECTION C: KEY CONCEPTS FROM PDF MISSING FROM QUESTION BANK (GAPS)
================================================================================

CHAPTER 1 GAPS:
---------------
1. FATF 40 RECOMMENDATIONS: The PDF (p.15) mentions FATF issued 40 recommendations
   and revised them in 2023. No question tests this specific number or revision year.

2. FATF STYLE REGIONAL BODIES: India is a member of APG and EAG (PDF p.16).
   This is mentioned in explanations but not directly tested as a question.

3. IMF AML ROLE: The PDF (p.16) discusses IMF's role in AML/CFT with 189 member
   countries and its expansion of AML efforts in 2000 and CFT after 9/11. No question
   covers this.

4. BIS (BANK FOR INTERNATIONAL SETTLEMENTS): The PDF (p.16-17) discusses BIS's
   role in AML. Founded 1930, oldest international financial institution. No question
   covers this.

5. UAPA 1967 DESCRIPTION: The PDF (p.18) describes UAPA specifically. No question
   in Chapter 1 tests this directly (it is covered in Ch.4 scheduled offences).

6. FEMA 1999 ROLE: The PDF (p.18) describes FEMA's role in foreign exchange
   regulation and preventing ML/TF through forex transactions. Not directly tested.

7. MULE ACCOUNTS: The PDF (p.13) defines "mule account" per SEBI PFUTP
   Regulations. This is a specific definition not covered by any question.

8. CHAIN-HOPPING, MIXING/TUMBLING, CYCLING: The PDF (p.13-14) describes these
   modern layering techniques for digital currencies. Question 3 tests layering generally
   but not these specific modern techniques.

9. SHELL COMPANIES: Mentioned as a placement method (p.13) - "establishing
   inactive companies or corporations that exist only on paper." Not directly tested.

CHAPTER 2 GAPS:
---------------
1. SECTION 2 DEFINITIONS - BENEFICIAL OWNER: The statutory definition of
   "beneficial owner" in Section 2(fa) (p.22) is not directly tested.

2. SECTION 2 - "CLIENT" DEFINITION: Section 2(ha) defines "client" (p.22).
   Not directly tested.

3. SECTION 2 - "REPORTING ENTITY" FULL DEFINITION: While Q10 tests what is NOT a
   reporting entity, the complete definition including "persons carrying on designated
   business or profession" details (casinos, real estate agents, precious metals dealers)
   from Section 2(sa) is not thoroughly tested.

4. SECTION 2 - "PROPERTY" DEFINITION: The broad definition of property (p.26) -
   corporeal/incorporeal, movable/immovable, tangible/intangible - is not tested.

5. SECTION 2 - "OFFENCE OF CROSS BORDER IMPLICATIONS" (ra): The definition on
   p.24 covering both directions (outside-to-India and India-to-outside) is not tested.

6. SECTION 11A - VERIFICATION OF IDENTITY: Listed in the sections table (p.28)
   but not tested.

7. SECTION 13 - POWERS OF THE DIRECTOR: Listed but not tested.

8. SECTION 14 - PROTECTION FROM CIVIL/CRIMINAL PROCEEDINGS: This is an important
   section - no civil or criminal proceedings against reporting entity, its directors &
   employees for furnishing information in good faith. Mentioned in Q18 explanation
   but not tested as a standalone question.

9. SECTION 44 - OFFENCES TRIABLE BY SPECIAL COURTS: Listed but not directly tested.

10. DESIGNATED DIRECTOR FOR OTHER ENTITY TYPES: The question tests company (MD/
    whole-time Director) but not partnership (managing partner), proprietorship
    (proprietor), trust (managing trustee), or unincorporated association roles.

CHAPTER 3 GAPS:
---------------
1. SMALL ACCOUNTS: The PDF (p.44-45) extensively describes small accounts - opened
   with self-attested photo, 12-month initial validity, monthly/annual transaction limits,
   no foreign remittances. This is a detailed topic with NO question coverage.

2. KYC DOCUMENTS FOR DIFFERENT ENTITY TYPES: Rule 9 sub-rules (4)-(9) detail
   specific documents for individuals (Aadhaar, PAN), companies (CoI, MoA/AoA, PAN),
   partnerships, trusts, and unincorporated associations. No question tests these
   specific document requirements.

3. RULE 9(1A) - KYC FILING WITH CKYCR: Filing within 10 days after commencement
   of account-based relationship. Not tested.

4. RULE 9(1B-1H) - KYC IDENTIFIER SYSTEM: The process of CKYCR issuing KYC
   Identifiers and the detailed update/retrieval mechanism. Not tested.

5. ONGOING DUE DILIGENCE - Rule 9(12): Three clauses on ongoing monitoring,
   review of due diligence, and applying CDD to existing clients. Not tested.

6. RISK ASSESSMENT - Rule 9(13): Requirement to carry out risk assessment and
   documentation thereof. Not tested.

7. DIGITAL KYC PROCEDURE: Section 3.3 (p.53-55) describes the detailed digital KYC
   process including live photograph requirements, watermarking, OTP validation,
   authorized officer declarations. No question coverage.

8. RULE 3A - GROUP POLICIES: While Q12 tests this, the specific mention of
   "tipping-off" prevention in group policies is not directly tested.

9. RULE 8(4) - EACH DAY OF DELAY = SEPARATE VIOLATION: This penalty provision
   is mentioned in Q4 explanation but not tested as a standalone question.

10. BENEFICIAL OWNER FOR TRUSTS: The PDF specifies "author of the trust, trustee,
    beneficiaries with 10% or more interest" (p.44). The trust threshold is tested
    only indirectly.

11. NON-PROFIT ORGANISATION REGISTRATION ON DARPAN PORTAL: Rule 9(9A) requires
    registration on NITI Aayog DARPAN portal. Not tested.

12. UTILITY BILL AND OTHER ADDRESS PROOF DOCUMENTS: Rule 9(18) lists specific
    documents for address proof (utility bill not more than 2 months old, etc.).
    Not tested.

13. AADHAAR REDACTION REQUIREMENT: Rule 9(16) requires clients to redact/black
    out Aadhaar number when authentication is not required. Not tested.

14. NON-PROFIT ORGANISATION TRANSACTIONS > Rs. 10 LAKH: Rule 3(BA) requires
    reporting of NPO transactions above Rs. 10 lakh. Not directly tested as a question.

CHAPTER 4 GAPS:
---------------
1. SPECIFIC BNS SECTION NUMBERS: The PDF maps IPC sections to BNS sections
   (e.g., IPC 302 = BNS 103(1) for murder). No question tests specific section
   number mappings.

2. PARAGRAPH-BY-PARAGRAPH COVERAGE: The question bank covers Paragraphs 1, 2,
   4, 5, 8, 11, 25, 29 but misses:
   - Paragraph 3: Explosive Substances Act, 1908
   - Paragraph 6: Wildlife Protection Act, 1972
   - Paragraph 7: Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956
   - Paragraph 9: Explosives Act, 1884
   - Paragraph 10: Antiquities and Arts Treasures Act, 1972
   - Paragraph 12: Customs Act under Part A (Section 135 - evasion)
   - Paragraphs 13-28: Various other Acts

3. PART B - SECTION 132 SPECIFICS: The Customs Act Section 132 covers "false
   declaration, false documents." This specific section is not tested.

4. PART C - OFFENCES AGAINST PROPERTY UNDER IPC CHAPTER XVII: Part C(3)
   specifically includes these offences with cross-border implications. Not tested.

5. PAGE 58 THRESHOLD NUANCE: The narrative mentions "3 million or 10 million
   rupees" for Part B, suggesting different thresholds for different sub-offences.
   This nuance is not explored.
