SECTION A: CORRECTIONS NEEDED
========================================

1. Primary Markets_16.xml
   ISSUE: The correct answer states private placement is "an offer of securities
   made to a select group of not more than 50 identified investors."
   PROBLEM: The PDF (page 106, section 3.17) states: "According to the Companies
   Act, 2013, an offer to subscribe to securities is made to not more than 50
   persons is called private placement of securities." The answer text says
   "50 identified investors" but the PDF says "50 persons." While conceptually
   similar, the precise legal language is "persons" not "investors." More
   importantly, the correct answer conflates the definition. The PDF defines it
   as an offer made to "not more than 50 persons" under the Companies Act, but
   the broader SEBI/market definition describes it as an offer to "a select
   group of institutional investors and other eligible investors" which is the
   QIB/wholesale market concept. The 50-person limit is the Companies Act
   threshold, while SEBI's ICDR regulations define private placement more
   broadly for QIP etc.
   FIX: Change the correct answer to: "An offer of securities made to a select
   group of not more than 50 persons as defined under the Companies Act, 2013"
   to match the PDF's precise language ("persons" not "investors"). Alternatively,
   simply say "An offer to subscribe to securities made to not more than 50
   persons" to mirror the workbook language.
   SEVERITY: Minor (wording precision)

2. Primary Markets_19.xml
   ISSUE: The question asks "Which document is filed with SEBI before a book-built
   public issue opens?" and the correct answer is "Draft Red Herring Prospectus
   (DRHP)."
   PROBLEM: The PDF (page 94, section 3.10) says "The draft prospectus is filed
   with SEBI" as a step in the public issue process. For book-built issues, the
   PDF (page 97) says "A preliminary red herring prospectus is filed with SEBI
   before the issue opens." The document filed with SEBI is technically the
   "preliminary red herring prospectus" not the "Draft Red Herring Prospectus
   (DRHP)." However, in common market practice the DRHP and preliminary RHP are
   used interchangeably. The PDF also states on page 94: "The draft prospectus
   is filed with SEBI." The question's explanation correctly describes the
   sequence but the PDF's own terminology uses "draft prospectus" and
   "preliminary red herring prospectus" rather than "DRHP."
   FIX: This is acceptable as-is since DRHP is the widely understood market
   term. However, the explanation could note that the PDF uses "preliminary red
   herring prospectus" language. No critical change needed.
   SEVERITY: Very minor (terminology nuance, functionally correct)

3. Primary Markets_29.xml
   ISSUE: The correct answer says minimum subscription is "90 percent of the net
   offer to the public." The explanation says "including subscription by
   underwriters."
   PROBLEM: The PDF (page 89, section on Underwriting) states: "SEBI's
   regulations on public issues and the Companies Act require that an issue
   should receive subscription of a minimum of 90 percent of the net offer to
   the public, failing which the company has to refund the entire subscription
   amount received to the applicants not later than 4 days from the closure of
   the issue." And later (page 96): "For an issue that is underwritten, SEBI's
   minimum subscription requirement is 90 percent of the net public offer
   including the subscription by the underwriters."
   The answer and explanation are correct per the PDF. CONFIRMED FINE.
   (Re-classified below.)

   VERIFICATION: PDF page 89 confirms: "refund the entire subscription amount

   (Re-classified below.)

5. Primary Markets_30.xml
   ISSUE: The correct answer says allotment in an oversubscribed IPO is "On a
   proportionate basis based on the oversubscription ratio, with minimum
   allotment being the minimum application size."
   PROBLEM: The PDF (page 99-100) describes proportionate allotment for
   oversubscribed issues. However, in practice (and acknowledged in the
   explanation), when proportionate allotment would result in less than one
   lot per applicant, a lottery system is used. The explanation hedges this
   ("While lottery may be used for very small lot sizes..."). The PDF does
   not explicitly mention lottery; it only describes proportionate allotment
   with the statement "The minimum allotment will be the minimum application
   size as disclosed by the issuer." The answer as stated is correct per the
   PDF text.
   FIX: No change needed to the answer. The explanation's hedging about lottery
   is reasonable context but should note this is practical implementation, not
   explicitly stated in the workbook.
   SEVERITY: None (answer is correct per PDF)

6. Primary Markets_41.xml
   ISSUE: The correct answer states NII allocation is "One-third for
   applications of Rs. 2-10 lakhs and two-thirds for applications above
   Rs. 10 lakhs."
   VERIFICATION: PDF page 93 states: "one-third of the portion available to
   non-institutional investors shall be reserved for applicants applying for
   amounts more than Rs. 2 lakhs and up to Rs. 10 lakhs; and the remaining,
   i.e., two-thirds of the portion shall be reserved for applicants with
   application size of more than Rs. 10 lakhs."
   The answer matches the PDF precisely. CONFIRMED FINE.

7. Primary Markets_46.xml
   ISSUE: The explanation states EBP is compulsory for "private placements of
   debt of Rs. 50 crores or more."
   VERIFICATION: PDF page 108 states the EBP is compulsory for "a single issue,
   for an amount equal to or greater than Rs. 50 crores (inclusive of green
   shoe option)." Also for shelf issues cumulatively amounting to Rs. 50 crore
   or more in a financial year. CONFIRMED FINE.

8. Primary Markets_17.xml
   ISSUE: The explanation states "No SEBI filing required like in a public
   issue" for QIP.
   PROBLEM: The PDF does not explicitly state this. The PDF (page 107) says QIP
   is defined by SEBI ICDR Regulations. While it is true that QIP does not
   require a prospectus filing with SEBI like a public issue does, the
   explanation's characterization could be misleading. The PDF is silent on
   this specific point.
   FIX: Consider removing "No SEBI filing required like in a public issue"
   from the Distributor's Pro-Tip to avoid potential inaccuracy, or qualify it
   as "does not require SEBI-reviewed prospectus like a public issue."
   SEVERITY: Minor (explanation only, not the answer itself)

9. Primary Markets_8.xml
   ISSUE: The explanation says "underwriting is discretionary for fixed price
   issues but mandatory for book-built issues."
   VERIFICATION: PDF page 89 states: "Underwriting for a fixed price issue is
   discretionary... However, in the case of a book built issue, underwriting is
   mandatory by the lead managers and syndicate members." And page 96 repeats:
   "Underwriting for a fixed price issue is discretionary. However, in the case
   of a book-built issue underwriting is mandatory."
   The explanation matches the PDF. However, the PDF adds an important nuance on
   page 89: "Under book-built issue, the issuer is required to allot at least
   75 percent of the net offer to Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIB) to meet
   the eligibility criteria; however, such QIB portion cannot be underwritten."
   The explanation omits the fact that the QIB portion cannot be underwritten.
   FIX: Consider adding to the explanation that in book-built issues, while
   underwriting is mandatory, the QIB portion cannot be underwritten.
   SEVERITY: Minor (explanation enhancement, answer is correct)

SECTION C: CONCEPT GAPS
========================================

The following important concepts from Chapter 3 of the PDF are NOT covered by
any of the 50 questions. These represent potential exam topics that should be
addressed:

1. MINIMUM OFFER TO PUBLIC THRESHOLDS (PDF p.88)
   GAP: No question tests the tiered minimum public offer requirements:
   - At least 25% if post-issue capital <= Rs. 1,600 crore
   - At least Rs. 400 crore equivalent if post-issue capital is Rs. 1,600-4,000 crore
   - At least 10% if post-issue capital is Rs. 4,000 crore to Rs. 1 lakh crore
   - Must increase to 25% within 3 years from listing date
   IMPORTANCE: HIGH. These are frequently tested thresholds.

2. MINIMUM NET WORTH REQUIREMENT FOR IPO (PDF p.86)
   GAP: While Q38 covers the Rs. 3 crore net tangible asset requirement, no
   question specifically tests the separate Rs. 1 crore net worth requirement
   per year for 3 years.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. Often tested alongside the other eligibility criteria.

3. SR EQUITY SHARES (Superior Rights) IN IPO CONTEXT (PDF p.87-88)
   GAP: No question covers the concept that companies with SR equity shares
   must list both SR and ordinary shares, and can only issue ordinary shares
   to the public through IPO on the Main Board.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. This is a newer regulation (2019 amendment).

4. EXCESS PROMOTER HOLDING LOCK-IN PERIODS (PDF p.87-88)
   GAP: While Q31 covers the minimum promoter contribution lock-in (18 months
   / 3 years for capex), no question tests the lock-in for EXCESS promoter
   holding: 6 months normally, or 1 year if majority proceeds are for capex.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. The four-tier lock-in structure is a common exam topic.

5. ALTERNATIVE ENTITIES FOR MEETING PROMOTER CONTRIBUTION SHORTFALL (PDF p.87)
   GAP: No question covers the provision that AIFs, foreign venture capital
   investors, scheduled commercial banks, PFIs, insurance companies, or
   non-individual public shareholders holding >= 5% can contribute up to 10%
   of post-issue capital to meet promoter contribution shortfall.
   IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM. Specific provision from 2024 amendment.

6. RIGHTS ISSUE OPEN PERIOD (PDF p.102)
   GAP: No question tests the rights issue subscription period: not less than
   7 days and not exceeding 30 days. The explanation in Q20 mentions it but
   no standalone question tests it.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. Frequently compared with public issue period (3-10 days).

7. NON-COMPETITIVE BIDDING FOR GOVERNMENT SECURITIES (PDF p.105-106)
   GAP: No question covers the non-competitive bidding facility for retail
   investors in government securities auctions through stock exchanges, or
   the distinction between competitive and non-competitive bidding.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. Important for understanding government securities market.

8. COMPANIES ACT REGULATORY FRAMEWORK FOR ISSUERS (PDF p.85-86)
   GAP: No question tests the requirement that a business seeking equity
   capital must be structured as a limited company, or that sole proprietorships
   and partnerships cannot make public issues.
   IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM.

9. e-IPO FACILITY (PDF p.98)
   GAP: No question covers the e-IPO facility introduced from 2013 under which
   investors can bid through any exchange broker's electronic trading facility.
   IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM.

10. INFORMATION CONTENT OF PROSPECTUS (PDF p.96-97)
    GAP: No question tests the specific contents required in a prospectus
    (details of issuer, objectives, financial statements, risk factors, etc.)
    or the grounds on which SEBI can reject a draft offer document (promoter
    contribution non-compliance, unclear usage of proceeds, misleading business
    model, financial inconsistencies, litigation issues).
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM-HIGH.

11. CREATION OF SECURITY FOR DEBENTURES (PDF p.104)
    GAP: No question covers the distinction between secured and unsecured
    debentures, the requirement to disclose charge/security proposals, or that
    unsecured debentures are treated as deposits under Companies Act rules.
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM.

12. SHELF PROSPECTUS ELIGIBLE ENTITIES (PDF p.103)
    GAP: While Q27 covers the concept of shelf prospectus, no question tests
    which entities are eligible to file one (PFIs, scheduled banks, entities
    allowed by CBDT for tax-free bonds, Infrastructure Debt Funds, listed
    entities, NBFCs, Housing Finance Companies meeting criteria).
    IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM.

13. FIXED PRICE ISSUE ALLOCATION (PDF p.93)
    GAP: No question tests the allocation for issues made through a fixed
    price (non-book-built) method: minimum 50% to retail individual investors,
    remaining to individual applicants other than retail and other investors.
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. Often compared with book-built allocation.

14. PUBLIC ISSUE OF DEBT - WILLFUL DEFAULTER RESTRICTION (PDF p.104-105)
    GAP: No question tests the requirement that an issuer of debt securities
    should not be declared a willful defaulter or barred from accessing the
    capital market.
    IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM.

15. PRICE BAND ANNOUNCEMENT TIMING (PDF p.98)
    GAP: While Q23 covers price band significance, no question specifically
    tests the requirement that the price band must be announced at least 2
    working days before the issue opens.
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. A specific numerical threshold likely to be tested.

16. MINIMUM BID LOT / APPLICATION VALUE RANGE (PDF p.98)
    GAP: No question tests the SEBI-prescribed minimum application value
    range of Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 15,000 for book-built issues.
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM. Important threshold for exam purposes.

17. QIP LISTING REQUIREMENT (PDF p.107)
    GAP: No question tests the requirement that a company must have been
    listed for at least 1 year before making a QIP, or that QIP shares can
    only be sold on a recognized stock exchange within 1 year of allotment.
    IMPORTANCE: MEDIUM.

18. EBP BIDDING LIMIT (PDF p.109)
    GAP: No question tests the EBP bidding limit: an eligible participant
    cannot bid for more than Rs. 100 crore or 5% of the base issue size,
    whichever is lower, through arrangers on the EBP platform.
    IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM.

19. CHANGED NAME RULE FOR IPO/FPO (PDF p.86, p.91)
    GAP: No question tests the rule that if a company changed its name
    within the last 1 year, at least 50% of revenue for the preceding 1
    year must have been earned from the activity indicated by the new name.
    IMPORTANCE: LOW-MEDIUM.
