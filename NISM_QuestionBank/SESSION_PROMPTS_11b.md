### Key Content Areas by Module

**Module 7: Risk Management and Insurance (Ch 1-3, 25 marks)**
- Insurance principles: insurable interest, utmost good faith (uberrimae fidei), indemnity, subrogation, contribution, proximate cause
- Life insurance: term, whole life, endowment, ULIP, money-back, annuity plans
- Life insurance needs analysis: HLV (Human Life Value), income replacement, expense method
- Non-life: health, motor, home, travel, crop insurance
- IRDAI regulations, claim settlement ratios
- Married Women's Property Act (MWPA) — Section 6 trust
- Comparison criteria for insurance products

**Module 8: Retirement Planning (Ch 4-6, 30 marks)**
- Retirement corpus calculation (present value, future value, inflation adjustment)
- NPS: Tier I (mandatory), Tier II (voluntary), asset classes (E/C/G/A), fund managers, exit rules
- EPF/PPF/Gratuity/Superannuation
- Annuity types: immediate vs deferred, fixed vs variable, life vs joint life
- Reverse mortgage
- Retirement planning life stages
- Philanthropy concepts

**Module 9: Taxation (Ch 7-13, 20 marks) — CALCULATION HEAVY**
- Residential status: ROR, RNOR, NR (182-day, 60+365 day rules)
- Five heads of income: salary, house property, PGBP, capital gains, other sources
- Capital gains: STCG vs LTCG, holding periods, cost of acquisition, indexation (CII)
- Section 80C, 80CCD, 80D, 80E, 80G deductions
- Clubbing of income (spouse, minor child)
- Set-off and carry forward of losses (inter-head, intra-head, 8-year carry forward for business losses)
- Equity STCG: 20%, LTCG: 12.5% above Rs. 1.25 lakh
- Debt MF (post Apr 2023): slab rate, always STCG
- DTAA: residence tie-breaker, tax credit methods, POEM
- NPS taxation: EET (exempt-exempt-taxed), 60% lump sum exempt

**Module 10: Estate Planning (Ch 14-15, 20 marks)**
- Wills: types (privileged, unprivileged), registration, codicil, probate
- Succession laws: Hindu Succession Act, Indian Succession Act, Muslim Personal Law
- Trusts: private vs public, revocable vs irrevocable, SEBI InvIT/REIT trusts
- Gifts: HUF, clubbing implications, stamp duty
- Power of Attorney: general vs specific
- Nomination vs succession

**Module 11: Behavioral Finance (Ch 16-17, 10 marks)**
- Biases: overconfidence, anchoring, loss aversion, herding, recency, confirmation, disposition effect, mental accounting, status quo, framing
- Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky): S-shaped value function, losses weigh more
- Nudge theory
- Role of adviser in managing client emotions

**Module 12: Comprehensive Financial Advice (Ch 18-20, 45 marks)**
- Risk profiling: willingness vs ability, psychometric tools, risk capacity vs risk tolerance
- Product comparison across categories (equity/debt/insurance/NPS/real estate/gold)
- Case studies: comprehensive financial plans integrating all modules
- This module should have the MOST caselets (heaviest weightage)
