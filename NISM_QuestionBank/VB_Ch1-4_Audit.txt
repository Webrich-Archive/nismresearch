A1. VB Concept and Role of Mutual Fund_12.xml
ISSUE: Minimum SIP amount stated as Rs. 500; PDF says Rs. 500 but also mentions
Rs. 50 and Rs. 100 as starting amounts for some investments.
DETAIL: The question says "As low as Rs. 500 per month" as the correct answer.
The PDF (Ch.1, section 1.2) mentions "There are some avenues where an investor can
start investing amounts as small as Rs. 50 or Rs. 100". While Rs. 500 is a common
SIP minimum across most AMCs, the question's explanation itself says "Some even
offer SIPs starting at Rs. 100" which contradicts the correct answer of Rs. 500.
FIX: Minor issue. Either change the correct answer to "As low as Rs. 100 per month"
or adjust the question to say "typically" or "commonly" Rs. 500. The explanation
already acknowledges Rs. 100 SIPs exist, so the answer should be more precise.
SEVERITY: LOW - The answer is practically correct for most funds, but the
explanation contradicts it.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A2. VB Concept and Role of Mutual Fund_2.xml
ISSUE: This question about mutual fund structure as a trust belongs more to
Chapter 3 (Legal Structure) than Chapter 2 (Concept and Role). While the PDF does
mention the trust form in Ch.2 section 2.1 ("Mutual fund is a vehicle (in the form
of a trust)..."), the detailed legal treatment is in Ch.3.
DETAIL: The question is factually correct - mutual funds are structured as trusts
under Indian Trusts Act, 1882 per PDF page 46.
FIX: No factual correction needed. Consider re-categorizing to Legal Structure
topic for better topical alignment, or keep as-is since Ch.2 does reference it.
SEVERITY: INFORMATIONAL ONLY

A3. VB Legal Structure of Mutual Funds_8.xml
ISSUE: AMFI described as a "self-regulatory organization" in the explanation.
DETAIL: The PDF (Ch.3, page 59) explicitly states: "An important point to note here
is that AMFI is neither a regulatory body nor a Self-Regulatory Organisation (SRO)."
The explanation says AMFI is "the industry body (self-regulatory organization)".
FIX: Remove "(self-regulatory organization)" from the explanation. Replace with
"the industry association" or "the industry body (note: AMFI is neither a regulator
nor an SRO)". This is a direct contradiction of the PDF.
SEVERITY: HIGH - The explanation contains factually incorrect information that
directly contradicts the workbook.

A4. VB Legal Structure of Mutual Funds_16.xml
ISSUE: Question says "The Trustees" appoint the AMC.
DETAIL: The PDF (Ch.3, page 50) states: "The sponsor or, the trustees if so,
authorized by the trust deed, shall appoint the AMC with the approval of SEBI."
So the appointment can be by the sponsor OR the trustees. The question's answer
"The Trustees" is not fully accurate - the sponsor can also appoint the AMC.
FIX: Change the correct answer to "The Sponsor or Trustees (with SEBI approval)"
or add nuance. Alternatively, keep the answer but fix the explanation to acknowledge
the sponsor's role too. The PDF also states on page 47: "The AMC is appointed by
the sponsor or the Trustees."
SEVERITY: MEDIUM - The answer is partially correct but oversimplified.

A6. VB Legal Structure of Mutual Funds_11.xml
ISSUE: The hierarchy "Sponsor -> Trust (Trustees) -> AMC -> Custodian/RTA" is a
reasonable simplification but the PDF structure is more nuanced.
DETAIL: Per PDF page 47, the AMC is appointed by "the sponsor or the Trustees."
The custodian is "appointed by the Trustees" (page 51), not by the AMC. So
Custodian is not really "below" AMC in the hierarchy - it's appointed by Trustees
directly.
FIX: Consider adjusting the correct answer to better reflect that the Custodian is
appointed by the Trustees (not the AMC). The current linear hierarchy is a
simplification. However, for exam purposes, this ordering is commonly used.
SEVERITY: LOW - Acceptable simplification for exam purposes.

PDF includes postal agents (no 10-year requirement mentioned for them) and retired
bank officers too.

add "retired teachers" as a category since the PDF does not list teachers. The PDF
says "class III and above or equivalent" for government officials - this detail is
also missing.
SEVERITY: MEDIUM - The explanation adds "retired teachers" which is NOT in the PDF.
This could mislead exam takers.

CHAPTER 1 - INVESTMENT LANDSCAPE:
Gap 1: FOUR BROAD ASSET CLASSES
  The PDF explicitly identifies four broad asset categories: Real Estate,
  Commodities, Equity, and Fixed Income (with Real Estate and Commodities as
  "real assets" and Equity and Debt as "financial assets"). No question directly
  tests this four-way classification.

Gap 2: FOUR BROAD FINANCIAL NEEDS
  The PDF identifies four financial needs: (1) Transaction needs, (2) Protection
  needs, (3) Investment needs, (4) Retirement needs. No question covers this
  framework specifically.

Gap 3: INTERNATIONAL ASSETS / CURRENCY RISK
  The PDF discusses international investments and exchange rate risk (page 17).
  No question covers this concept.

Gap 4: INFLATION CALCULATION FORMULA
  The PDF provides a case study (Sapna's education, page 20) using the formula
  Future Value = Present Value x (1 + inflation rate)^years. While Q14 tests
  inflation impact, no question uses the compound inflation formula from the
  PDF's example (Rs. 10 lac becoming Rs. 21.59 lac at 8% over 10 years).

Gap 5: REAL ESTATE CHARACTERISTICS
  The PDF discusses real estate as an asset class extensively (illiquidity, high
  ticket size, maintenance costs, difficulty of purity assessment in gold). No
  question tests real estate or commodity characteristics specifically.

Gap 6: COST OF SELF-MANAGING vs. MUTUAL FUND
  PDF section 1.5 discusses three key questions about mutual fund choice: Can one
  do it oneself? Does one want to? Can one afford to outsource? This framework is
  not tested.

  The PDF lists 5 categories: (A) Equity (11 sub-categories), (B) Debt (16
