RRP-Q8 (Spread Risk): MINOR ISSUE
  Question says spread risk is "the risk that the yield difference between corporate bonds
  and government securities may widen."
  PDF says (Sec 10.1.2, Floating Rate Securities): "Spread Risk: In a floating rate security
  the coupon is expressed in terms of a spread or mark up over the benchmark rate. In the
  life of the security this spread may move adversely leading to loss in value of the portfolio."
  The PDF defines spread risk specifically in the context of FLOATING RATE SECURITIES, not
  as a general credit-spread widening concept. The question's definition is more of a
  "credit spread risk" concept which is related but not how the PDF specifically frames it.
  RECOMMENDATION: Revise to align with the PDF's floating-rate-specific context, or
  explicitly note this is credit spread risk vs. the spread risk in floaters.

RRP-Q10 (Basis Risk): ACCEPTABLE BUT IMPRECISE
  Question defines basis risk in terms of "derivative may not move in perfect correlation
  with the underlying asset being hedged."
  PDF says (Sec 10.1.2): "Basis Risk arises due to a difference in the price movement of
  the derivative vis-a-vis that of the security being hedged." -- This matches.
  PDF also defines basis risk for floating rate securities: "The underlying benchmark of a
  floating rate security or a swap might become less active or may cease to exist."
  STATUS: Correct for derivatives context. No change needed.

RRP-Q12 (Cash Drag): NOT DIRECTLY IN PDF
  The concept of "cash drag" is not explicitly named in the PDF. The PDF discusses that
  "open-ended schemes maintain a part of their portfolio in liquid assets" and that "the
  liquid assets component in the portfolio of an equity fund can dilute the returns" (Ch 12,
  Sec 12.3). The concept is implied but the term "cash drag" is not used.
  RECOMMENDATION: Add a note in the explanation that this is an implied concept, not
  an explicitly named one in the NISM workbook. The answer is factually sound.

   PDF discusses this extensively with examples (80/20 for moderate, diversified funds

    LOW PRIORITY GAP.

SECTION D: CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES
================================================================================

1. CHAPTER ALIGNMENT
   Several questions in the "Risk Return and Performance" set actually test Ch 11 concepts
   (Sharpe, Treynor, TRI). Similarly, Scheme Selection questions test Ch 2/5 (SEBI
   categorization norms) and Ch 8 (taxation) concepts. While cross-chapter testing is
   acceptable, the topic labels may confuse students studying chapter-by-chapter.

2. CONCEPTS NOT IN PDF
   The following concepts appear in questions but are NOT in the PDF Chapters 10-12:
   - R-squared (RRP Q21, Q38, Q41, Q45)
   - Sortino Ratio (SP Q8, Q22, Q31)
   - Up-Capture / Down-Capture Ratio (SP Q13, Q23, Q24)
   - Correlation as portfolio construction tool (RRP Q45)
   These are all valid finance concepts but students relying solely on the NISM workbook
   would not have encountered them. Consider either removing them or marking them
   clearly as supplementary material.
   COUNT: ~10 questions (7.4% of total) test beyond-workbook concepts.

3. CALCULATION-HEAVY DISTRIBUTION
   Risk Return and Performance: 15 calculation questions out of 50 (30%)
   Scheme Performance: 8 calculation questions out of 35 (23%)
   Scheme Selection: 7 calculation questions out of 50 (14%)
   The distribution is reasonable. The NISM exam typically includes some calculations.

4. DIFFICULTY DISTRIBUTION
   All three sets progress from foundational to applied to case-study questions.
   This is a good pedagogical structure.
